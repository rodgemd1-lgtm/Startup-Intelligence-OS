# Behavioral Economics Module

Every Susan agent applies behavioral economics principles through the BE Lens.

## The 12 BE Mechanisms

1. **Loss Aversion** — Losses feel 2-2.5x worse than equivalent gains (Kahneman & Tversky)
2. **Endowment Effect** — People overvalue what they already "own"
3. **Sunk Cost** — Past investment increases commitment to continue
4. **Status Quo Bias** — Preference for current state; change feels risky
5. **IKEA Effect** — Things you helped build feel more valuable
6. **Social Proof** — Following others' behavior in uncertain situations
7. **Commitment/Consistency** — Acting consistently with stated commitments
8. **Default Effect** — Pre-selected options are chosen more often
9. **Anchoring** — First number seen influences subsequent judgments
10. **Framing Effect** — Same info, different presentation = different decisions
11. **Present Bias** — Overvaluing immediate rewards vs future ones
12. **Hyperbolic Discounting** — Steep discounting of delayed outcomes

## LAAL Protocol (Loss-Aversion Acquisition Loop)

Every product interaction should create:
1. **Ownership Asset** — Something the user considers "theirs"
2. **Cost of Leaving** — What they'd lose (progress, identity, social, asset)
3. **Minimum Return Action** — Under 2 minutes to re-engage
4. **Return Reward** — Immediate value for coming back
5. **Investment Flywheel** — Each interaction increases switching cost

## Loss Framing Protocol

Default to LOSS FRAMING for all re-engagement copy:
- BAD: "Come back and earn 50 points!"
- GOOD: "Your 847-point streak expires in 23 hours"
- BAD: "Try our new workout feature!"
- GOOD: "Your custom plan is losing calibration without new data"

## Copy Templates by Trigger

- **Dormant D3**: "Your [asset] is still here, but [time-decay element]..."
- **Dormant D7**: "Your [progress metric] is starting to [loss verb]..."
- **Dormant D14**: "[Social proof] users who returned at this point recovered [X]%..."
- **At-Risk**: "You've built [specific progress]. Without [action], [specific loss]..."

## Agent BE Map

Every agent must apply the BE lens before finalizing output:
1. LOSS AUDIT: Does this help users feel the cost of NOT acting?
2. OWNERSHIP CHECK: Does the user own something that makes leaving feel like a loss?
3. IDENTITY ALIGNMENT: Does this reinforce who they're becoming?
4. FRICTION AUDIT: Is the return threshold under 2 minutes?
5. PROGRESS VISIBILITY: Is progress visible, named, and personalized?
