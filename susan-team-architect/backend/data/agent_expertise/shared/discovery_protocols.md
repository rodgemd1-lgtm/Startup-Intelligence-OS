# Discovery Protocols

Great operators do not jump from request to solution. They diagnose the job, the resistance, the emotional stakes, and the proof threshold first.

## 5 Whys
- Why does the user say they want this?
- Why does that matter right now?
- Why is the current behavior, tool, or surface failing?
- Why does this matter emotionally, socially, or professionally?
- Why would they trust or distrust the proposed answer?

Use the fifth why to reveal the hidden variable:
- identity threat
- status threat
- uncertainty
- fear of wasted effort
- fear of regret
- fear of being judged

## Context before content

For coaching, orchestration, customer success, and any longitudinal interaction:

- ask one grounded life-context question before prescribing action
- reflect back the context before moving to goals
- prove that the recommendation fits the person's real constraints

This is where remembered detail becomes useful rather than decorative.

## Relational discovery

Use a light relational scan before deep task design:

- what is filling the user's day right now?
- who or what is competing with this goal?
- what detail would make the user feel understood rather than processed?
- what detail would feel too intimate too early?

Good relational design increases trust. Bad relational design creates uncanny-valley surveillance.

## JTBD frame
Map every request into:
- functional job: what they need done
- emotional job: how they need to feel while doing it
- social job: how they want to be perceived
- switching pain: what makes change feel costly
- success signal: what proof tells them the job is done

## Moments of truth
Every experience has a few moments where belief changes faster than everywhere else.

Map at minimum:
- first impression
- first comprehension
- first trust test
- first commitment ask
- first friction or failure
- first success
- return moment

For each moment, ask:
- what is the user feeling?
- what are they afraid of?
- what proof do they need?
- what should the system amplify?
- what should the system remove?

## Need router
Use the diagnosis to pull the right science and methods:
- cognitive load and attention control
- behavioral economics and motivation
- self-determination and identity formation
- trust and risk reduction
- accessibility and inclusive interaction
- enterprise proof architecture
- clinical or compliance caution

## Value triage
Separate:
- real value: changes comprehension, trust, action, or outcomes
- perceived value: looks impressive but may not improve decisions
- novelty value: feels fresh now but decays quickly
- durable value: will still matter after the trend wave passes

## Operating rule
Before proposing a concept, answer:
- What job is actually being hired for?
- What emotion must be transformed?
- What moment of truth matters most?
- What evidence or design move will change belief?
