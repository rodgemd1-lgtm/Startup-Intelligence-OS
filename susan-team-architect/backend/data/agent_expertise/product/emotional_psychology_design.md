# Emotional Psychology for Product and Web Experience

## Core States

- Anxiety
- Hope
- Relief
- Aspiration
- Pride
- Shame risk
- Belonging
- Skepticism

## Design Implications

- Anxious users need lower information density, calmer pacing, and trust transfer.
- Aspiring users tolerate higher drama if it still feels credible.
- Skeptical users need proof before flourish.
- Shame-sensitive contexts must avoid manipulative urgency and exaggerated before/after framing.

## Practical Methods

- Align the first screen with the user's emotional state, not the company's feature list.
- Use pacing to regulate arousal.
- Reserve motion emphasis for uncertainty reduction, proof sequencing, and commitment reinforcement.
- Use typography and whitespace to signal seriousness or care.

## Moments of Truth

- First fold
- Proof reveal
- Pricing reveal
- Account creation
- Post-click reassurance
- Recovery moments after missed progress or failure

## Failure Modes

- High-energy visuals for high-anxiety decisions
- Growth copy that intensifies shame
- Over-rational interfaces in emotionally charged categories
- Mood inconsistency across adjacent screens
