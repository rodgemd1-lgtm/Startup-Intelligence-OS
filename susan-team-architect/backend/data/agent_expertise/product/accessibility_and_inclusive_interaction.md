# Accessibility and Inclusive Interaction

## Required Standards

- Reduced-motion support for all non-essential animation
- Keyboard and screen-reader coverage for primary actions
- Contrast and legibility that survive real conditions, not just mockups
- Touch targets sized for fatigue, motion, and limited dexterity

## Inclusion Rules

- Accessibility is not a QA pass at the end; it changes layout and interaction choices up front.
- Design for older adults, injured users, low-vision users, and cognitively overloaded users.
- Alternative flows should preserve dignity, not feel second-class.

## Review Questions

- Does the experience still work without motion?
- Does the hierarchy survive when effects are disabled?
- Are urgent states understandable without color alone?
- Can a first-time user complete the path under stress?
