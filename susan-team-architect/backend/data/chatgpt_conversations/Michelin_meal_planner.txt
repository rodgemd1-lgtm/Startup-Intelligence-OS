2. French Beef Tenderloin Dinner
Main: Roast Beef Tenderloin with Shallot–Red Wine Reduction


Ingredients (adapted from Williams‑Sonoma)


1 beef tenderloin (2½–3 lb)

1 tsp freshly ground pepper


2 Tbsp minced shallots


1 cup full‑bodied red wine such as Cabernet Sauvignon


2½ Tbsp unsalted butter




Method


Let beef rest at room temperature for one hour.  Preheat oven to 450 °F.


Rub tenderloin with olive oil, thyme, salt and pepper and place on a V‑shaped rack in a roasting pan.  Roast until an instant‑read thermometer reads 125 °F for rare (about 20 min) or 130 °F for medium‑rare (25 min).  Transfer to a board; tent with foil and rest 15 min.


For the sauce, place the pan over medium heat, add shallots and sauté until translucent.  Add red wine gradually, scraping up browned bits, and reduce by half.  Stir in butter until emulsified and keep warm.


Slice tenderloin ½ inch thick, arrange on a platter and drizzle with reduction.

1. Crispy Duck Breast, Potato Pavé & Roasted‑Beet Salad
Main: Pan‑Roasted Duck Breast with Cherry–Port Reduction
Ingredients (serves 4)
ItemQuantityDuck breasts (with skin)4 × 6–8 ozKosher salt & black pepperto tastePitted dark cherries1 cupPort wine*½ cupChicken stock½ cupShallot, minced1Fresh thyme sprig1Unsalted butter1 Tbsp

Method


Prepare and sear the duck: Pat duck breasts dry.  Score the skin in a crosshatch pattern without cutting into the meat and season generously with salt and pepper.  Place skin‑side down in a cold, oven‑safe skillet and heat over medium.  Render the fat and crisp the skin for ~7 minutes until deeply golden; spoon off excess fat as needed.  Flip and sear the flesh side for 1 minute.


Roast and rest: Transfer the skillet to a 400 °F (205 °C) oven and roast until an instant‑read thermometer registers 135 °F (57 °C) for medium‑rare, about 5–7 minutes.  Rest the duck on a cutting board, tented loosely with foil, for 5 minutes.


Make the cherry–port reduction: Pour off most of the rendered fat, leaving about 1 Tbsp in the pan.  Add the minced shallot and sauté until translucent.  Deglaze with port and add cherries, thyme and stock.  Simmer until reduced by half and cherries are soft.  Remove the thyme, whisk in butter and season to taste.


Serve: Slice the duck breasts and fan over the potato pavé.  Spoon cherry reduction over the top.
