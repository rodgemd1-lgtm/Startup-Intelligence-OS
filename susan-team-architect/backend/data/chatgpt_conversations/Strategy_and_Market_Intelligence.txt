Objective and audience
Working assumption: you want a research-backed reference model for what a modern Strategy + Market + Competitive Intelligence department should include, aimed at an executive audience deciding charter, scope, roles, and operating model.
Key message
A good strategy / market / competitive intelligence department is not a report factory, an annual-planning admin team, or a sales battlecard shop. It is a closed-loop decision system: frame the right strategic questions, continuously sense markets/customers/competitors, turn signals into choices, translate choices into initiatives and budgets, and keep testing assumptions after the decision is made. APQC treats market/customer intelligence, competitor analysis, opportunity prioritization, pricing, and channel strategy as formal business processes; McKinsey’s recent strategy research says the biggest performance gap is in mobilization, not just strategy design; and HBS’ Office of Strategy Management work makes the same point by positioning a small central team as the integrator of budgeting, operational planning, and performance management. Red Solutions Hub+2McKinsey & Company+2
A second non-obvious point: market intelligence is not the same thing as market research. Market intelligence is continuous external sensing; market research is often a one-off answer to a defined question. Modern departments need both. Mintel draws that distinction directly, McKinsey emphasizes real-time customer understanding and demand visibility, and ESOMAR’s 2025 buyer research shows almost half of research projects are now done internally, with external providers increasingly acting as partners rather than simple vendors.
