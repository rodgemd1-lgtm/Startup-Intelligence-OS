ASSISTANT: You’ve got a 12" Staub enameled cast iron pan. Important: Staub interiors are matte black enamel, not raw cast iron — so you do not season it like a Lodge skillet. No flax oil bake-offs needed.
The good news: if it’s sticking, it’s almost always buildup or technique — not lack of seasoning.

✅ The Easy Fix (No Oven, No Smell)
What you want is a conditioning oil, not traditional seasoning.
👉 Best Simple Option (Wipe On / Heat On Stovetop)
Crisbee Rub Cast Iron & Carbon Steel Seasoning

Why:


Super easy

Small tin lasts forever


How to Use (5 minutes total)


Wash pan well (Bar Keepers Friend if there’s buildup).


Dry completely.


Rub a very thin layer of Crisbee inside.


Put on stovetop medium heat 5–7 minutes.


Let cool. Wipe excess.


That’s it. No oven required.

🔥 Even Easier (Zero “Seasoning”)
If you want zero hassle:
Use avocado oil or grapeseed oil before cooking every time.
High smoke point + preheating properly = nonstick-ish performance.
Most sticking on enameled cast iron is from:


Not preheating long enough


Moving food too early


Cooking too low heat


Let proteins naturally release.

If You Do Want Oven Seasoning
If you ever decide to oven cure (rarely needed for Staub):
To reduce smell:


Use very thin coat


Line bottom rack with foil


Open windows + turn on vent fan


400°F instead of 450°F


45–60 minutes max


But again — you really don’t need to oven-season enamel.

If it’s matte black (normal Staub interior), don’t try to build thick seasoning layers. It won’t bond like raw iron.
