USER: How do rental cars like enterprise get great reviews? Do they focus on getting more reviews or fixing bad reviews

---

ASSISTANT: Mostly both, but the flywheel usually starts with operational consistency first, then review volume second, then recovery on bad reviews.
For rental car brands like Enterprise, great reviews usually come from a few boring things done well:
What actually drives ratings


Fast pickup and drop-off


Car is clean and actually available


Staff solves problems on the spot


Billing is clear and there are no surprise fees


Local branch manager cares about service, not just transactions


A rental car company can’t “review-strategy” its way out of bad operations for long. If the wait is awful, the car class is wrong, or the bill feels sketchy, more review asks just scale the disappointment.
Key message
The best performers usually fix the experience first, then systematically ask happy customers for reviews, and actively recover unhappy ones before they post.
Conservative / base / aggressive
1) Conservative: focus on fixing bad reviews
What it is: Triage complaints, resolve branch-level issues, respond to negative reviews fast.
Pros


Improves real root causes


Prevents repeated damage


Builds trust internally


Cons


Slow visible improvement in star rating


Doesn’t generate much new positive volume


Expected outcome


Better reputation over time


Lower complaint recurrence


Small but real lift in ratings


Best when


Current experience is inconsistent


Lots of 1–3 star reviews mention the same issues


Confidence: High

2) Base: fix root causes and increase review volume
What it is: Improve service moments that matter most, then ask satisfied customers for reviews at the right moment.
Pros


Most balanced approach


Ratings improve faster


Better reflects true customer base, not just angry outliers


Cons


Requires branch discipline


Needs simple measurement and follow-through


Expected outcome


Stronger average rating


More representative review mix


Better local branch performance


Best when


Experience is decent but underrepresented online


You have many happy customers who never leave reviews


Confidence: Very high

3) Aggressive: engineer the whole reputation system
What it is: Build branch-level service metrics, review requests, escalation workflows, manager incentives, and rapid service recovery.
Pros


Fastest improvement


Creates a repeatable reputation engine


Lets you identify weak branches quickly


Cons


Can get fake/manipulative if done badly


More operational overhead


Needs strong governance


Expected outcome


Higher review count


Better ratings


Better branch accountability


Best when


You run multi-location operations


Reviews materially affect demand


Confidence: High
Recommendation
Go with the base approach.
Why:


If you only chase more reviews, you amplify a broken experience.


If you only fix bad reviews, you leave the public story dominated by a small set of complainers.


The winning move is: remove the biggest service failures, then capture more of the quiet happy majority.


What smart operators usually do
They focus on:


Eliminating review killers: long lines, wrong vehicle, dirty car, billing surprises


Asking at the peak moment: right after a smooth drop-off or problem resolution


Local accountability: branch managers own service scores


Service recovery: catch unhappy customers before they become public detractors


Volume with integrity: ask everyone or a broad set of customers, not just cherry-picked fans


Simple rule


If your average experience is weak: fix bad reviews first


If your average experience is solid but review count is low: push review generation


If you want great reviews at scale: do both, in that order


Risk log


Asking for more reviews too early can surface more negative reviews


Responding to bad reviews without fixing ops does nothing


Over-incentivizing staff can create awkward or manipulative asks
