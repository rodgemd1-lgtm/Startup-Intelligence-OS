# Feeling Data and Emotional Signals

Feeling data is qualitative evidence about emotional state, not just preference.

## What Counts as Feeling Data

- Exact phrases users use when describing fear, motivation, shame, hope, or relief
- Facial or bodily cues observed in interviews or usability sessions
- Where users pause, reread, scroll back, or hesitate
- What kinds of proof calm them down versus overload them
- Which visual and verbal choices make the product feel credible, premium, safe, or energizing

## Operationalizing Feeling Data

For each touchpoint, document:

1. Entry emotion
2. Desired exit emotion
3. Triggers that elevate tension
4. Signals that lower resistance
5. Copy, layout, and motion choices that support the transition

## Common Emotional States in Startup Journeys

- Skeptical but curious
- Overwhelmed and tired
- Hopeful but embarrassed
- High intent but risk-sensitive
- Ambitious and identity-driven
