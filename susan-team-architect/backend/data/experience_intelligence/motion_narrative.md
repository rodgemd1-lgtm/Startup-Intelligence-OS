# Motion Narrative for Conversion and Product Experience

Motion is not decoration. It is sequencing.

## Core Principle

Every transition should answer one of four questions:

1. What should the user notice now?
2. What emotional state are we trying to create or regulate?
3. What should feel inevitable, not abrupt?
4. What uncertainty is the motion dissolving?

## Good Motion Narrative

- Reveals one dominant idea at a time
- Slows down at high-trust moments
- Speeds up through obvious confirmations
- Uses motion to transfer confidence, not show cleverness
- Supports an emotional arc: curiosity -> trust -> desire -> commitment -> reassurance

## Failure Modes

- Motion that adds spectacle but no clarity
- High-velocity interfaces at moments where the user needs calm
- Generic fade-and-slide systems that flatten emotional cadence
- Over-animating proof, pricing, or health claims in ways that feel manipulative
