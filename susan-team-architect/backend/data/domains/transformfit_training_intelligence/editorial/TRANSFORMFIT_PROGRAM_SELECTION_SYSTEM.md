# TransformFit Program Selection System

TransformFit should recommend programs from athlete reality, not from aesthetic preference alone.

## Selection Inputs

- training age
- primary goal
- days per week realistically available
- equipment access
- session time budget
- recovery reliability
- motivation and adherence risk

## Selection Matrix

| Situation | Default Program | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| true beginner, full gym, 3 days | Full Body Foundations | highest movement practice, lowest complexity |
| intermediate, wants size and strength, 4 days | Upper Lower Power Hypertrophy | strong mixed adaptation with manageable fatigue |
| intermediate, high adherence, 6 days | Scientific Push Pull Legs | maximizes specialization and weekly volume |
| busy adult, full gym, 3 days, 45-55 min | Efficient Hypertrophy 3-Day | preserves stimulus without overcommitting frequency |
| home lifter, dumbbells only | Dumbbell Momentum 3-Day | preserves progression with limited equipment |

## Redirection Rules

- If adherence is uncertain, bias down in frequency before biasing down in ambition.
- If equipment is limited, preserve movement intent before preserving exercise names.
- If recovery is weak, cut volume before intensity on compounds.
- If the athlete misses more than one week, restart with a re-entry week, not with the planned current week.

## Coaching Implications

- Week one should explain why this split fits the athlete's life.
- After missed sessions, the coach should reduce shame and present a clear on-ramp.
- The product should show why swaps preserve progress rather than feeling random.

## Evaluation Questions

- Did the athlete understand why they got this program?
- Did week one feel challenging but survivable?
- Did the first missed-session recovery preserve momentum?
- Did the program still feel intelligent after the first swap?
