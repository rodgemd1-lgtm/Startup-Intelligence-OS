# Oracle Health Studio Memory Seed

## Current strengths
- strong four-layer intelligence model: market, workflow, regulatory, and narrative
- good foundation for workflow-proof marketing
- strong opportunity to create a research-to-asset operating system

## Active lessons

### Lesson 1
Generic enterprise AI language is not enough for Oracle Health narratives.

Implication:
- workflow proof, implementation realism, and buyer-safe phrasing should anchor all assets

### Lesson 2
Executive and buyer-facing assets become stronger when interoperability is framed as usable workflow rather than standards compliance alone.

Implication:
- show operational consequences, not just architecture or policy concepts

### Lesson 3
Decks, memos, white papers, and articles should descend from one research spine.

Implication:
- do not produce standalone assets without shared proof and thesis

## Seed experiments

### Experiment A
- hypothesis: workflow-first deck framing will outperform AI-first deck framing with enterprise buyers
- variable: opening section emphasis
- success signal: stronger buyer resonance and clearer follow-up questions

### Experiment B
- hypothesis: screenshot-backed workflow proof will outperform copy-only enterprise messaging
- variable: annotated screenshot insertion in decks and white papers
- success signal: stronger credibility and reuse across asset types

## Reusable rules
- operational proof before category language
- one research spine, many assets
- compliance-sensitive claims should be reviewed early
- buyer trust rises when the narrative feels implementation-aware
