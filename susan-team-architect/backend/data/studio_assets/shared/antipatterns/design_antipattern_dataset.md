# Design Anti-Pattern Dataset
## Initial Failure Library For The Cognitive Design Studio

Each anti-pattern includes:
- symptom
- why it fails
- detection signals
- rescue move

---

## Anti-Pattern 1: Premium AI Glow Soup

### Symptom
Dark background, generic gradients, glow, glass, and vague copy intended to signal premium intelligence.

### Why it fails
- feels interchangeable
- adds atmosphere without proof
- often hides weak hierarchy and generic message architecture

### Detection signals
- could swap the logo and keep the same page
- hero says "AI-powered" without concrete behavior
- glow and glass do more work than screenshots or proof

### Rescue move
- replace visual abstraction with one product truth
- add screenshot or behavioral proof
- reduce effects until hierarchy and argument carry the page

---

## Anti-Pattern 2: Feature-Stack Hero

### Symptom
The hero opens with 3-6 features, multiple buttons, badges, and visual modules.

### Why it fails
- comprehension fragments immediately
- no one belief is established
- the user is asked to parse before feeling oriented

### Detection signals
- more than one real headline idea above the fold
- multiple competing visual centers
- buttons appear before trust is earned

### Rescue move
- reduce to one belief shift
- one primary CTA
- move supporting proof into secondary layers

---

## Anti-Pattern 3: Motion As Anesthetic

### Symptom
Heavy motion is used to create perceived quality where the concept is weak.

### Why it fails
- motion delays understanding
- performance and accessibility suffer
- the page feels more expensive than trustworthy

### Detection signals
- motion could be removed with no change in argument
- everything fades or flies in the same way
- reduced-motion fallback feels broken or flat

### Rescue move
- define the job of each animated sequence
- remove anything that does not change understanding, trust, or pacing

---

## Anti-Pattern 4: Glass Over Dense Proof

### Symptom
Material depth and blur are layered under dense copy, tables, or proof blocks.

### Why it fails
- readability drops
- trust falls because information feels stylized instead of grounded

### Detection signals
- dense content on translucent or visually noisy surfaces
- users need more effort to parse evidence

### Rescue move
- use solid or nearly solid proof surfaces
- confine material effects to chrome, nav, or support layers

---

## Anti-Pattern 5: Onboarding Interrogation

### Symptom
The product asks many personalization questions before delivering any meaningful value.

### Why it fails
- commitment is demanded before value is legible
- setup fatigue increases abandonment

### Detection signals
- more than a few high-effort questions before first output
- users cannot tell why each question matters

### Rescue move
- compress setup
- delay noncritical questions
- show first value before asking for deeper disclosure

---

## Anti-Pattern 6: Progress Invisibility

### Symptom
The product provides activity but not a felt sense of progress or adaptation.

### Why it fails
- users do not build ownership
- return motivation weakens

### Detection signals
- users complete sessions but cannot articulate what is changing
- progress is trapped in hidden dashboards or abstract numbers

### Rescue move
- add explicit progress signals
- show interpretation, not just data
- make progress visible in the first week
