# Marketing Anti-Pattern Dataset
## Initial Failure Library For The Cognitive Marketing Studio

---

## Anti-Pattern 1: Format-First Content

### Symptom
The team asks for a deck, memo, or white paper before defining the belief that needs to change.

### Why it fails
- produces assets without message clarity
- creates content sprawl

### Detection signals
- no audience-specific belief shift
- no explicit proof threshold

### Rescue move
- define audience, belief shift, emotional resistance, and proof before format

---

## Anti-Pattern 2: Proofless Differentiation

### Symptom
The narrative claims uniqueness without visible evidence.

### Why it fails
- buyers dismiss it as branding language
- the team starts compensating with volume and adjectives

### Detection signals
- "leading", "transformative", or "reimagined" with no operational proof
- no screenshots, workflows, examples, or credible references

### Rescue move
- make every major claim carry visible proof
- use screenshots, examples, workflows, or grounded data

---

## Anti-Pattern 3: Generic Enterprise AI Language

### Symptom
The asset uses fashionable AI language that could belong to any vendor.

### Why it fails
- destroys trust quickly with sophisticated buyers
- removes ownable narrative territory

### Detection signals
- if a competitor name could replace yours with no structural rewrite
- heavy use of "transformation", "intelligence", or "innovation" without specifics

### Rescue move
- anchor in workflow, implementation, or buyer consequence
- write the sentence the buyer should remember and defend

---

## Anti-Pattern 4: Derivative Sprawl

### Symptom
The team creates many assets that do not descend from one research spine.

### Why it fails
- message drift
- duplicated work
- conflicting proof across assets

### Detection signals
- each asset has a different thesis
- slides, blogs, and memos do not reinforce each other

### Rescue move
- establish one narrative spine
- plan the derivative cascade before production

---

## Anti-Pattern 5: Objection Blindness

### Symptom
The asset states the team's story but never resolves the buyer's real hesitation.

### Why it fails
- the narrative feels self-referential
- buyers leave with the same unanswered question

### Detection signals
- no explicit objection map
- FAQs and appendix material carry the real argument instead of the main asset

### Rescue move
- identify the hardest objection first
- place proof and explanation where resistance peaks

---

## Anti-Pattern 6: Compliance Afterthought

### Symptom
The team writes the message and only later asks whether the phrasing is safe.

### Why it fails
- rewrites get expensive
- trust language becomes unstable late in the process

### Detection signals
- regulated or healthcare claims appear before review
- legal constraints surface after design and content are already locked

### Rescue move
- include claim-risk review in the studio workflow
- route early to Shield or compliance review when needed
