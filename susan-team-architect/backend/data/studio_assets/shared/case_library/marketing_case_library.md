## Case 3: Decision Memo Before Long-Form Asset

### Context
Teams often jump directly into white papers or presentations without first locking the decision frame.

### Job solved
- clarify the recommendation before expanding into broader content
- force proof and objection handling early

### Why it works
- memos expose weak logic quickly
- it is easier to critique recommendation quality than deck polish
- a good memo becomes the source material for slides and articles

### Reusable patterns
- memo first for ambiguous strategy topics
- recommendation -> reasons -> proof -> objections -> next action

### Do not copy blindly
- memo-first when a fast campaign asset is all that is needed

### Context

### Job solved

## Case 5: Screenshot-Led Enterprise Narrative

### Context
In B2B and healthcare, buyers increasingly need to see workflow reality, not just positioning statements.

### Job solved
- make abstract claims feel concrete
- increase trust in implementation credibility

### Why it works
- screenshots ground the story
- annotations direct attention toward workflow value
- visuals help bridge product, marketing, and sales

### Reusable patterns
- one workflow screenshot per major belief claim
- annotate only the operationally important step
- pair with short buyer-relevant interpretation

### Reuse when
- the audience is skeptical, operational, or enterprise-facing
