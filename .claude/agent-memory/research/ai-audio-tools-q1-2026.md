## Key Facts to Remember

### Copyright Landscape (as of Q1 2026)
- Suno settled with Warner Music (November 2025) — new licensed models coming 2026
- Udio settled with UMG and WMG (October–November 2025) — joint licensed platform coming 2026
- Sony lawsuit vs Udio still ongoing
- AI-generated music is NOT automatically protected by copyright in the US (human authorship requirement)
- Most music generators: paid subscribers own commercial rights, free users do not

### Top Performers by Category
- Best voice quality: ElevenLabs (Eleven v3), Cartesia Sonic-3
- Best real-time latency: Cartesia Sonic Turbo (40ms), ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 (75ms)
- Best film/TV voice work: Respeecher (Emmy-winning)
- Best audio repair: iZotope RX 11 Advanced
- Best noise cancellation: Krisp (sub-20ms latency)
- Best stem separation quality: LALAL.AI (Demucs v4 backend)
- Best music generation for sync: Suno v5, AIVA Pro
- Best STT accuracy: Deepgram Nova-3 (5.26% WER)

### Status Flags
- Coqui TTS: company shut down December 2025; codebase maintained as community fork
- Amper Music: discontinued as standalone; integrated into Shutterstock
- Google MusicFX (formerly MusicLM): free via AI Test Kitchen; Lyria 2 in private beta
- Harmonai/Dance Diffusion: low-activity, research-grade only
- Riffusion: open-source, limited to short clips
