**Owner**: Mike Rodgers
**Version**: 1.0 APPROVED
**Last Updated**: 2026-03-24
**Category**: Startup / Venture Operations
**Priority**: P1 — Partnerships can 10x distribution overnight; bad partnerships can drain 6 months
**Maturity**: Documented (this SOP)
**Applies to**: TransformFit brand partnerships, Alex Recruiting institutional relationships, technology integrations, and co-marketing

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the structured process for identifying, evaluating, structuring, and managing partnerships — from initial outreach to ongoing relationship governance. This SOP prevents Mike from entering misaligned partnerships out of excitement and ensures that every active partnership has clear mutual value and defined success metrics.

**Core principle**: A partnership is only valuable if it is asymmetrically good for both parties. If you'd be embarrassed to show the partner what you're getting out of the deal, it's not a good partnership — it's a one-sided favor.

**Framework basis**: Steve Blank's partnership tiering for startups, Nir Eyal's engagement loop for platform partnerships, and McKinsey's alliance management methodology adapted to early-stage ventures.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 In Scope

- Distribution partnerships (access to each other's audiences)
- Technology / API integrations (e.g., integrating a nutrition database, wearable API)
- Co-marketing partnerships (joint content, cross-promotion)
- Channel partnerships (gyms, fitness facilities selling TransformFit)
- Institutional relationships (Alex Recruiting: high school networks, D3 conferences)
- Affiliate and referral partner programs

### 2.2 Out of Scope

- Oracle Health vendor relationships (separate enterprise procurement process)
- Investor relationships (SOP-35 governs investor communications)
- Contractor and freelancer relationships (SOP-32)
- Sponsorships received (sponsorship management is a separate workflow)

## 3. PHASE 1: Partnership Qualification

### 3.1 The Partnership Hypothesis

Before pursuing any partnership, complete the hypothesis:

```
PARTNERSHIP HYPOTHESIS
-----------------------
Potential partner: [Name / company]
Partnership type: Distribution / Tech / Co-marketing / Channel / Institutional

WHAT WE GET:
- Access to: [specific audience, technology, channel]
- Estimated impact: [X new users / $X revenue / Y% retention improvement]
- Uniqueness: [Can we get this elsewhere? If yes, why them?]

WHAT THEY GET:
- Access to: [what we offer them]
- Estimated value to them: [honest estimate]
- Why would they say yes?: [their business motivation]

ALIGNMENT TEST:
- Do our audiences overlap but not conflict? YES / NO
- Do our business models align (not compete)? YES / NO
- Is the timing right for them? YES / UNKNOWN / NO

MINIMUM GO THRESHOLD:
- We will pursue if: [specific condition — e.g., "if they have X audience size AND are open to CPA model"]
- We will not pursue if: [specific disqualifier]
```

### 3.2 Partnership Tier Framework

| Tier | Type | Effort | Potential Impact | Examples |
|------|------|--------|-----------------|---------|
| **T1 — Strategic** | Deeply integrated, co-branded, exclusive | High (100+ hours) | Business-changing | TransformFit × major fitness brand co-launch |
| **T2 — Growth** | Audience sharing, co-marketing | Medium (20–50 hours) | Significant growth | Instagram fitness influencer × affiliate |
| **T3 — Tactical** | Referral program, integration, link swap | Low (2–10 hours) | Incremental | API integration, newsletter cross-promo |

**Rule**: Mike has capacity for maximum 1 active T1 partnership, 3 T2 partnerships, and unlimited T3 at any time. Overcommitting to partnerships kills execution speed.

## 4. PHASE 2: Outreach and First Contact

### 4.1 Research the Partner Before Outreach

For any T1 or T2 pursuit, complete pre-outreach research:

| Research Area | Questions to Answer |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Business model | How do they make money? Are we aligned or in conflict? |
| Audience | How large? How engaged? How similar to our target? |
| Partnership history | Have they partnered before? On what terms? Any public learnings? |
| Current priorities | What are they trying to achieve in the next 6 months? |
| Decision maker | Who controls partnerships decisions? LinkedIn profile? |

### 4.2 Outreach Template (Warm Introduction Preferred)

**Via warm intro (always try this first):**

> "Hey [mutual contact], I'm exploring a partnership between TransformFit and [partner]. We think there's a real fit — [their audience] + [our product] could help [outcome]. Do you know [partner name]? Would you be comfortable making an intro?"

**Cold outreach (when warm intro unavailable):**

Subject: TransformFit x [Partner] — quick partnership question

> Hi [Name],
> 
> Quick ask — [brand] and TransformFit feel like a natural fit: you've built [their audience/asset], we've built [our asset]. I think [specific value proposition for them] — there could be a simple co-marketing deal here worth exploring.
> 
> 15 minutes this week or next? I have a specific structure in mind and don't want to waste your time.
> 
> Mike

**Key principle**: Don't pitch the full deal in the first message. Get the meeting.

## 5. PHASE 3: Partnership Structure

### 5.1 Common Partnership Structures

| Structure | How It Works | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Revenue share** | Partner earns % of revenue from referred customers | Affiliate channel, gym channel partners |
| **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)** | Mike pays fixed $ per user brought by partner | Influencer campaigns with measurable signup |
| **CPM (Cost Per Thousand impressions)** | Mike pays for audience exposure | Brand awareness, new market entry |
| **Equity / Advisor stake** | Small equity for strategic advice + network access | Key advisors who open major distribution |
| **Integration revenue split** | Revenue share on upsells enabled by integration | App store, platform add-on |
| **Product comp exchange** | Free/discounted product for cross-promotion | Early-stage when cash is tight |

### 5.2 Partnership Term Sheet (Lean Format)

For T1 and T2 partnerships, document the deal before starting:

```
PARTNERSHIP TERM SHEET
[Venture] x [Partner Name]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Status: DRAFT / AGREED / EXECUTED

PARTIES:
- [Venture]: [Mike's role]
- [Partner]: [Partner's name, title]

WHAT EACH PARTY DELIVERS:
[Venture] agrees to:
- [Specific deliverable 1 — dates, quantities]
- [Specific deliverable 2]

[Partner] agrees to:
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]

COMPENSATION / REVENUE SHARE:
[Model, rates, payment timing]

SUCCESS METRICS:
- Primary KPI: [metric — e.g., 500 new trial signups in 90 days]
- Review date: [when we evaluate if it's working]
- Exit condition: [what would trigger winding down the partnership]

EXCLUSIVITY:
- [None / Category exclusivity / Full exclusivity] for [duration]

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY:
- Each party retains IP in their own materials
- Co-created materials owned by: [joint / [Venture] / [Partner]]

TERM AND TERMINATION:
- Initial term: [X months]
- Renewal: [auto-renew / manual approval]
- Termination notice: [X days written notice]

GOVERNING LAW: [State]
```

## 6. PHASE 4: Partnership Operations and Health Monitoring

### 6.1 Active Partnership Dashboard

For every active T1 and T2 partnership, maintain a one-page status doc updated monthly:

```
PARTNERSHIP STATUS: [Venture] x [Partner]
Last updated: [date]

STATUS: 🟢 On Track / 🟡 At Risk / 🔴 Needs Attention

KPIs THIS MONTH:
- [Primary KPI]: Target: ___ / Actual: ___ / Trend: ↑↓→
- [Secondary KPI]: Target: ___ / Actual: ___

WHAT'S WORKING:
- [1-2 observations]

WHAT'S NOT WORKING:
- [1-2 observations]

JOINT ACTIONS DUE:
- [ ] [Action] — Owner: [who] — Due: [date]

RELATIONSHIP HEALTH:
Last contact: [date]
Communication quality: Good / Adequate / Needs attention
Renewal likelihood: High / Medium / Low
```

### 6.2 Partnership Review Cadence

| Tier | Review Frequency | Format |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| T1 Strategic | Monthly | 30-min video call + status doc |
| T2 Growth | Quarterly | Status doc + async update |
| T3 Tactical | Biannually | Quick email check-in |
