## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure defines the complete operating model for Oracle Health's Marketing & Competitive Intelligence (M&CI) function. It establishes what the department does, what it owns, how it operates, how it is measured, and how it creates value for Oracle Health's business.

SOP-26 is the meta-document. It is the answer to the question every new stakeholder, new hire, and senior leader should be able to ask and immediately receive: "What does M&CI actually do, and why does Oracle Health need it?"

This document covers:

- The 12 strategic areas the M&CI function spans
- Which of those areas are active today versus aspirational
- How time and capacity are allocated across those areas
- The operating calendar (annual, quarterly, monthly, weekly cadences)
- The stakeholder ecosystem M&CI serves
- Team design from solo operator to full department
- The full tool stack including Jake AI augmentation
- The budget model and ROI justification
- A quantitative Department Maturity Score (DMS)
- A Monte Carlo program ROI simulation
- Governance, decision rights, and escalation paths
- A 12-month build plan to reach full maturity
- Success metrics, KPIs, and RACI assignments

SOP-26 does not replace domain-specific SOPs (competitor profiling, battlecard creation, win/loss analysis, etc.). It frames and governs them. Every domain SOP exists as a child of SOP-26.

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

- All intelligence production activities owned or co-owned by the M&CI function at Oracle Health
- All stakeholder relationships where M&CI is a primary or supporting intelligence provider
- All tools, data sources, and automation systems used to produce intelligence
- Budget, headcount, and capacity planning for the M&CI function
- Governance and decision rights for intelligence outputs

### Out of Scope

- Oracle Health corporate strategy formulation (M&CI is an input function, not the strategy owner)
- Product roadmap decisions (M&CI provides intelligence; product owns the decisions)
- Sales execution and CRM management (M&CI provides battlecards and win/loss; sales owns the pipeline)
- Marketing campaign execution (M&CI provides market and demand intelligence; campaign ops owns execution)
- Legal, regulatory, and compliance decisions (M&CI surfaces regulatory signals; legal owns the decisions)

### Geographic Scope

United States healthcare IT market — primary. International markets — monitoring only unless a specific project is scoped.

### Organizational Scope

Oracle Health. Oracle Health's primary healthcare IT product lines and competitive set. Oracle Cerner legacy products are in scope as part of the competitive context where relevant.

The M&CI function transforms market noise into strategic clarity — delivering the intelligence Oracle Health's leaders need to win more, move faster, and make decisions they can defend.

We exist not to produce reports, but to change outcomes: to increase win rates, reduce strategic mistakes, and give Oracle Health an intelligence advantage over every competitor in the healthcare IT market.

## 4. The 12 Areas: Active vs. Aspirational

This section maps Oracle Health's M&CI against APQC's Process Classification Framework and SCIP's Competitive Intelligence Maturity Model, as benchmarked against McKinsey, BCG, and Bain CI function designs.

### Classification System

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **ACTIVE-CORE** | Fully operational. Output is produced regularly. Stakeholders actively consume it. |
| **ACTIVE-PARTIAL** | Operational but coverage is incomplete, inconsistent, or not yet systematized. |
| **ASPIRATIONAL-NEXT** | Designed and prioritized for build in the next 6 months. |
| **ASPIRATIONAL-HORIZON** | Planned for 7-18 month horizon. |

### Area 1: Strategic Agenda Alignment

**Definition**: The M&CI function maintains explicit alignment with Oracle Health's top 3-5 strategic priorities. Every intelligence output traces back to at least one strategic priority. Intelligence that does not map to a strategic priority is deprioritized.

**Status**: ACTIVE-PARTIAL

**Current State**: Mike has informal alignment with Matt Cohlmia and Seema's organizations. Strategic priorities are understood but the formal traceability mapping (intelligence output → strategic priority) is not yet documented.

**Target State**: Formal Strategic Agenda Map exists. Every recurring intelligence output has a documented strategic priority owner. Priority changes trigger a 30-day review of all active intelligence workstreams.

**APQC Reference**: Process 10283 — Develop and manage competitive intelligence strategy
**McKinsey Alignment**: CI functions that formally align to strategy agendas produce 2.3x more decision influence than those that don't (McKinsey Global Survey, Strategic Intelligence 2023)

**Key Activities**:
- Annual: Participate in Oracle Health strategic planning cycle; map M&CI coverage to top priorities
- Quarterly: Review strategic priority list; adjust intelligence coverage accordingly
- Monthly: Confirm active intelligence requests map to current strategic priorities
- On-demand: Respond to strategic priority shifts with rapid intelligence recalibration

**APQC Reference**: Process 10281 — Monitor competitor activities
**McKinsey Alignment**: Companies with active CI programs win 35% more competitive deals (McKinsey Commercial Excellence Survey 2024).

**Key Activities**:
- Annual: Full competitor profile rebuild; competitive landscape report
- Quarterly: Competitor update synthesis; battlecard refresh cycle; pricing intelligence update
- Monthly: Competitor signal digest; win/loss by competitor analysis
- Weekly: Signal monitoring via Jake pipeline; breaking news alerts; battlecard flags
- On-demand: Rapid response briefs (<24 hours) for deal-specific competitive intelligence

**Key Output Artifacts**:

### Area 6: Foresight & Scenario Planning

**Definition**: Forward-looking intelligence work — 3-5 year horizon analysis, scenario planning, war gaming, discontinuity analysis, and strategic early warning systems.

**Status**: ASPIRATIONAL-NEXT

**Current State**: No systematic foresight capability exists. Mike has strategic intuition and can produce ad hoc scenario analyses, but there is no structured methodology, no recurring scenario planning cadence, and no war gaming process.

**Target State**: Annual war game exercise (Oracle Health vs. Epic, Epic vs. AI-native disruptors). Quarterly scenario planning updates. Three named scenarios maintained for Oracle Health's 3-year horizon. Discontinuity watch list (10-15 potential discontinuities monitored).

**SCIP Reference**: Advanced CI programs produce formal scenario planning frameworks. Organizations at CI Maturity Level 4+ conduct annual war games.

**Build Priority**: Q3 2026. Requires: structured scenario planning methodology, stakeholder buy-in for war game exercises, at least 1 additional M&CI team member to facilitate.

**Key Activities (target state)**:
- Annual: War game exercise (2-day offsite); full scenario refresh
- Quarterly: Scenario update; discontinuity watch review
- Monthly: Early warning signal review
- On-demand: Rapid scenario brief for specific strategic questions

**Key Output Artifacts (target state)**:
- Oracle Health 3-Year Scenario Set (maintained)
- War Game Report (annual)
- Discontinuity Watch List (quarterly)
- Rapid Scenario Brief (on-demand)

        description = "Anticipating needs; high automation; growing adoption"
    else:
        tier = "Predictive"
        description = "AI-augmented; influencing strategy; full coverage"

    return {"dms": round(dms, 2), "tier": tier, "description": description}

## Appendix A: SCIP Maturity Model Reference

The Strategic and Competitive Intelligence Professionals (SCIP) maturity model defines five levels of CI program maturity:

| Level | Name | Characteristics |
|-------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Ad Hoc | Intelligence produced reactively, no process |
| 2 | Functional | Basic processes, some regular outputs |
| 3 | Integrated | Systematic production, stakeholder distribution, basic automation |
| 4 | Strategic | Intelligence embedded in strategy; proactive anticipation; high automation |
| 5 | Transformational | AI-augmented, fully integrated into all major decisions, industry benchmark |

**Oracle Health M&CI Current SCIP Level: 3.5 (between Integrated and Strategic)**

The DMS of 3.33 is consistent with the SCIP Level 3-4 range. The 12-month target of DMS 4.33 corresponds to SCIP Level 4 (Strategic).
