## WHY ORACLE WINS IN RCM

**1. We are the only vendor whose EHR IS the revenue cycle.**
Every other RCM solution — Waystar, R1, Ensemble, nThrive — sits outside the clinical record and requires an interface layer to pull charges, documentation, and coding data. Oracle Health generates charges from clinical documentation in real time, within the same system. No interface delay. No data translation error. No third-party latency. When a physician documents a service, the revenue cycle has already started. That's a structural advantage nobody else can buy or build quickly.

**2. Our AI Agents act on the complete patient record — not just billing codes.**
Waystar's Iodine CDI and R1's Phare OS work on claims data or downstream documentation. Oracle's AI Revenue Cycle Agents (launching March 31, 2026) run inside the EHR, with access to the full clinical record — lab values, problem lists, nursing notes, order history, genomic data. When flagging a documentation gap that will cause a denial, Oracle's AI knows why the patient is complex. Competitors are guessing from billing codes. We are reading the full chart.

**3. The CMS-0057-F rule effectively mandates what Oracle already does natively.**
The January 2026 prior authorization reform requires FHIR-based API connectivity between payers and providers by 2027. Oracle Health has TEFCA QHIN designation today. Our FHIR infrastructure is already built. Competitors that are clearinghouse-first (Waystar, Change Healthcare) will need to integrate with EHRs they don't own to meet this mandate. Oracle closes the loop without an extra vendor.
*Proof point: Oracle showcasing FHIR-native prior auth automation at HIMSS26, March 2026.*

**4. OCI is the most defensible infrastructure story in healthcare since Change Healthcare.**
After the 2024 Change Healthcare cyberattack exposed 190 million patients and paralyzed claims processing for months, healthcare buyers are asking a question they never asked before: "What happens to our cash flow if our RCM vendor gets attacked?" Oracle Cloud Infrastructure offers military-grade security, multi-region redundancy, and a no-single-clearinghouse-dependency architecture. Our answer to that question is better than any competitor's.

## WINNING DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

1. **"What percentage of your denied claims are you currently catching before submission versus after the payer rejects them?"**
   *Why it works:* Forces the buyer to quantify the gap. If they say "most are caught upfront," challenge gently with the industry data (62% of RCM leaders say denials are their #1 issue in 2026). If they say "mostly after rejection," you've just opened the door on Oracle's AI prevention story.

2. **"Walk me through what happens in your organization when a payer changes its prior authorization requirements. How fast do you know, and how fast does that change make it into your workflow?"**
   *Why it works:* This surfaces the real-time payer intelligence gap. Most health systems find out about payer rule changes weeks after they've started generating denials. Oracle's multimodal AI uses real-time CMS and payer rule data — not just historical claims patterns. This question isolates that differentiation.

3. **"If you add up the staff hours your team spends on denial management, appeals, and prior auth follow-up in a given week — what does that number look like?"**
   *Why it works:* Industry data says RCM teams spend 51–75 hours per week on denial-related work. Getting the buyer to say their number out loud creates the ROI anchor. Then you can show specifically which of those hours Oracle AI agents can absorb.
