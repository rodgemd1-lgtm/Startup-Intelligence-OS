## WHY ORACLE WINS IN PAYER

**1. We fix denials BEFORE they happen — no one else can.**
Oracle's AI agents (Prior Authorization, Coding, Claims, Eligibility) are embedded in the EHR at the point of care. Payer rules get applied when the clinician documents — not after the claim is rejected. Competitors like Cotiviti catch problems post-payment. We prevent them. Proof: AI-enabled coding reaches 95%+ accuracy; top deployments show 30-40% fewer denials.

**2. We are the only vendor with both sides of the payer-provider equation.**
Oracle Health operates in the provider EHR (clinical workflow) AND connects payers via Oracle Health Data Intelligence and Clinical Data Exchange. No other payer technology vendor has a live EHR + payer connectivity platform in production. Availity routes transactions. Cotiviti analyzes claims retrospectively. Zelis processes payments. Oracle is the only platform that touches clinical decisions in real time, on both sides.

**3. CMS-0057-F compliance is built in — not bolted on.**
Oracle's Prior Authorization Agent is FHIR-native. The Clinical Data Exchange supports Patient Access APIs, Payer-to-Payer exchange, and structured PA workflows — all CMS-0057-F requirements. Oracle's architecture was designed for this regulatory environment. Stopgap FHIR wrappers from competitors will break; Oracle's native FHIR stack will not.

**4. Oracle Cloud Infrastructure = the most secure healthcare cloud.**
After the 2024 Change Healthcare breach (the largest healthcare cyberattack in U.S. history), payers cannot accept single-point-of-failure clearinghouse risk. OCI is consistently ranked among the top enterprise security platforms globally. Oracle's distributed architecture eliminates the concentration risk that crippled Change Healthcare. Reference: Oracle Health Clinical Data Exchange has not experienced a Tier 1 security incident. Point made.

## WINNING DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

**Question 1 (Regulatory Opener):**
"CMS public PA performance metrics are due March 31 — eight days away. What is your average prior authorization turnaround time today, and how confident are you that those numbers are defensible in a public report?"

WHY IT WORKS: Forces the prospect to confront a dated, specific compliance reality. If they're struggling, you've opened the remediation conversation. If they're confident, pivot to: "Great — what's your plan for sustaining that performance at scale as PA volume grows?"

**Question 2 (Denial Burning Platform):**
"What percentage of your denied claims in 2025 were coding-related — and what would a 30% reduction in coding denials mean in recovered revenue for your organization?"

WHY IT WORKS: Converts a technology conversation into a finance conversation. Gets the CFO interested. Benchmarks the industry at 126% increase in coding denials over 3 years — the answer is almost always worse than they know.

**Question 3 (Competitive Moat):**
"Your providers use [Epic/Cerner/other EHR]. When a prior authorization is initiated, at what moment does payer clinical criteria get applied — during documentation, at order entry, or after submission?"

WHY IT WORKS: Exposes the gap between where clinical decisions happen and where payer rules are enforced. If the answer is 'after submission,' Oracle's left-shift model is the solution. If they say 'during documentation,' ask which system does it — it's almost never their current payer tech stack.

## LANDMINES

**LANDMINE 1: Oracle EHR Market Share Decline**
DO NOT lead with EHR market share stats. Epic routinely outperforms Oracle Health in new health system EHR wins. If the conversation goes to EHR competitive positioning, deflect to the payer-provider DATA layer. "Oracle Health Data Intelligence connects to any EHR — Epic, Cerner, athena. This isn't an EHR conversation."

WHY: Raising EHR competition invites the prospect to question Oracle's overall health trajectory, which is a distraction from the payer-specific value proposition where Oracle is genuinely strong.

**LANDMINE 2: Change Healthcare Breach Timing**
DO NOT say "Change Healthcare was breached, therefore Oracle is safer" without having the specific OCI security architecture brief ready. Making security claims without backup invites challenges you may not be prepared to answer.

WHY: Security claims require substance. If challenged on specifics (penetration testing, SLA uptime, data residency), an unprepared response damages credibility more than silence. Brief available from Oracle Health security team — request it before any payer CIO conversation.

**LANDMINE 3: Cotiviti 300 Million Member Claim**
Cotiviti's "serves 300 million members" stat sounds overwhelming. Do NOT try to beat them on member reach numbers — Oracle doesn't compete that way.

WHY: This is Cotiviti's strongest positioning statement. Countering with Oracle's member count invites a losing comparison. Redirect: "Cotiviti has broad payer analytics reach at the population level. Oracle works at the individual patient-encounter level, in real time. Different layers of the same problem."

## COMPETITIVE TRAPS THEY'LL SET

**TRAP 1: "Best in KLAS" — The Edifecs/Cotiviti Credential Ambush**

WHAT THEY DO: Cotiviti/Edifecs sales reps will open meetings by showing the 2026 KLAS certificate for CMS Payer Interoperability (score 91.9). They will frame the KLAS win as proof that they are the compliance-proven choice. Procurement teams who don't know KLAS will anchor on this credential.

THE COUNTER: "KLAS measures customer satisfaction and vendor performance, not clinical outcomes or workflow integration. Edifecs scores well because they reliably translate legacy EDI to FHIR — they're a strong infrastructure layer. Oracle Health isn't in that KLAS category because we operate at a different layer: clinical workflow, not data plumbing. Ask Cotiviti/Edifecs what their impact has been on PA turnaround time for a provider using Epic or Oracle Health EHR. That answer will reveal the gap."

Preparation: Request Oracle Health's VBC analytics and AI agent case studies showing PA turnaround improvement and denial rate reduction at named customers before any Cotiviti competitive situation.

**TRAP 2: The "Full Platform" UHG/Optum Sales Play**

WHAT THEY DO: UHG Optum reps will propose a "full end-to-end" platform play — UHC payer data + Optum claims intelligence + Change Healthcare clearinghouse + Optum Health care delivery. They will price it as a bundle and imply that fragmented "best-of-breed" approaches create integration overhead and security risk comparable to what caused Change Healthcare's 2024 failure.

THE COUNTER: "UHG's bundle is powerful if all of your volume runs through UHG-affiliated plans. For diversified payer portfolios — MA, Medicaid, commercial, Blues — concentrating on UHG's infrastructure means your data and operations are tied to a competitor's platform. After 2024, your board should be asking: do we want our clinical data pipeline inside a single organization that is simultaneously our largest payer competitor? Oracle is the neutral, non-payer platform. We don't compete for your members."

Preparation: Frame Oracle's multi-payer neutrality as a strategic advantage. Oracle Health never competes with payer clients for enrollment. Build this into executive presentations.
