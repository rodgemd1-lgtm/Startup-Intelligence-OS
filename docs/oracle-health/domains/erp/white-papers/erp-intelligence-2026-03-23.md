## Executive Summary

The global healthcare ERP market stands at approximately $9.01 billion in 2026 and is on a trajectory to reach $12.26–$15.7 billion by 2031–2033, growing at a compound annual rate of 6.35–8%. Despite this growth, the market remains structurally conservative: on-premises deployments still account for roughly 74% of the installed base, and finance/billing remains the primary functional entry point at 30.4% of total spend. Hospitals are the dominant buyer segment, representing 53.8% of total ERP revenue, and North America leads regionally with 38.6% of global market share.

The most consequential near-term signal is the convergence of two competitive displacement events: SAP's discontinuation of its IS-H healthcare-specific solution (directly impacting 60+ hospitals in DACH with ripple effects in North America) and Infor Lawson's confirmed 2030 end-of-life, which is already accelerating cloud migration timelines across its healthcare customer base. These two events alone represent thousands of seats in active evaluation or imminent displacement — an unprecedented window for Oracle.

Workday remains the most formidable competitor, having earned its ninth consecutive Best in KLAS award for ERP for Large Organizations in February 2026. However, Workday's supply chain capabilities are structurally incomplete, relying on third-party partnerships with Tecsys (WMS), Cargoson (TMS), and Scout/Evisort for procurement and contracts. Oracle's native, end-to-end SCM suite — delivered without add-on fees for AI agents or agentic workflows — represents a durable structural advantage in contested deals.

Health system operating margins remain critically thin (median 1.3% in December 2025), with supply expenses surging 12.3% YOY and drug costs climbing 6.1% YOY. This financial pressure is the primary catalyst for ERP investment, forcing CFOs and COOs to seek consolidated platforms that reduce total cost of ownership. Oracle's ability to deliver a single integrated suite spanning ERP, HCM, SCM, and — uniquely — clinical EHR integration positions the company ahead of any pure-play competitor.

The three strategic priorities for Oracle in the next six months: (1) aggressive pursuit of Infor Lawson healthcare accounts facing 2030 EOL urgency; (2) targeted displacement of SAP IS-H accounts in North America leveraging SAP's strategic retreat from healthcare-specific solutions; and (3) conversion of the large PeopleSoft installed base using Oracle's own upgrade narrative combined with demonstrated AI value.

### 1.2 Cloud Migration Dynamics

The healthcare ERP cloud transition is real but slower than other verticals:

- **On-premises still dominates**: 73.8% of the market in 2023, declining but persistent
- **Primary barrier**: Data governance, regulatory compliance (HIPAA, 21st Century Cures), and deep EHR integrations anchor organizations to on-premises
- **Hybrid models are the transition path**: Health systems want cloud economics with on-premises control — Oracle's hybrid cloud architecture directly addresses this
- **Infor community acceleration**: Among Infor users, active cloud migration has nearly doubled YOY (2026 survey), driven by the 2030 Lawson EOL signal

### 1.3 Key Market Drivers in 2026

1. **Margin compression**: Health system median operating margins at 1.3% in December 2025 (Strata Decision Technology). Supply expenses up 12.3% YOY, drug costs up 6.1% YOY. CFOs are mandated to find cost reduction through operational consolidation.

2. **AI at inflection point**: 80% of U.S. healthcare executives face board-level pressure for immediate AI ROI (KPMG 2025). AI in supply chain moving from proof-of-concept to proof-of-value. Platforms that embed AI without extra licensing fees have a structural pricing advantage.

3. **Platform consolidation mandate**: The "patchwork of legacy systems" problem (Billings Clinic-Logan Health's exact words, December 2025) is the dominant buying trigger. Health systems want one vendor, one contract, one upgrade cycle.

4. **Workforce management crisis**: Physician investment per FTE exceeded $343K in Q4 2025 (+11.5% YOY). Automated scheduling, credentialing, and staffing tools are now C-suite priorities, not IT priorities.

5. **Supply chain resilience post-pandemic**: The GHX 2026 predictions highlight cyber resilience, pharmacy supply chain modernization, and clinical alignment as the new supply chain performance metrics. Platforms must handle end-to-end traceability including medical device recall management.

## 2. Regulatory Landscape

### 2.1 CMS Cost Reporting & Modernization

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) is actively modernizing its data and financial infrastructure. In February 2026, Oracle Cloud Infrastructure was selected to support the CMS modernization initiative — a significant federal validation of Oracle's healthcare data architecture. For ERP sales, this creates a credible third-party endorsement: if the federal healthcare payer trusts Oracle OCI, health system CFOs face reduced procurement risk.

CMS cost reporting requirements mandate precise financial tracking at the service line, facility, and payer level. Oracle Fusion Cloud's hierarchical financial reporting structures, pre-built government reporting templates, and audit trail capabilities are directly aligned with CMS reporting obligations.

### 2.2 340B Drug Pricing Compliance

The 340B program allows covered entities (hospitals, FQHCs, RHCs) to purchase outpatient drugs at significant discounts. Compliance requires:
- Split billing: Tracking 340B-eligible vs. non-eligible prescriptions
- Contract pharmacy oversight: Maintaining audit trails across pharmacy partners
- Accumulator reporting: Documenting eligible patient encounters and drug utilization

Oracle's SCM module with pharmaceutical inventory tracking and contract management capabilities supports 340B compliance workflows. This is a competitive differentiator vs. Workday (which lacks native pharmaceutical SCM) and a key discovery question area.

### 2.3 HRSA Requirements

The Health Resources & Services Administration enforces 340B compliance audits and oversight. Health systems face increasing HRSA scrutiny in 2026, particularly after the 340B contract pharmacy expansion rulings. ERP systems must provide:
- Eligible patient documentation linked to drug purchasing
- Contract pharmacy network audit trails
- Real-time reporting of program eligibility

### 2.4 No Surprises Act & Good Faith Estimates

Oracle has integrated No Surprises Act compliance functionality into its healthcare ERP suite (confirmed in Oracle's regulatory compliance podcast series). This requires ERP systems to generate good-faith cost estimates for self-pay patients — a financial workflow that intersects patient access, billing, and supply chain costing.

### 2.5 21st Century Cures Act & Interoperability

ERP systems increasingly need to support interoperability requirements under the 21st Century Cures Act, particularly as ERP data (credentialing, supply chain, financial) intersects with clinical data flows. Oracle's unique position as the only vendor offering both EHR (Oracle Health/Cerner) and ERP from a single company creates compliance leverage here.

### 3.2 Workday — Primary Threat

Workday is the most credible competitor in healthcare ERP heading into Q2 2026. Their ninth consecutive KLAS Best in KLAS win for ERP for Large Organizations (February 2026) is the most-cited reference in competitive deals. Their unified HR+Finance platform resonates strongly with CMOs and CHROs.

**Where Workday is structurally weak:**
- Supply Chain Management: Depends on Tecsys (WMS partnership), Cargoson (TMS partnership), Scout (sourcing — acquired), Evisort (contracts — acquired but described as "incomplete" in Oracle's February 2026 comparison)
- Transportation Management: Not available natively; partner-dependent
- AI pricing: Workday charges extra for AI agent usage; Oracle includes agentic AI at no extra fee
- Gartner Source-to-Pay: Workday absent from leadership categories where Oracle is named Leader

**Workday's HIMSS presence** (booth #4208, Las Vegas, March 9-12, 2026) signals aggressive healthcare market push. Teams should expect post-HIMSS pipeline acceleration from Workday.

### 3.3 SAP — Strategic Retreat Creates Opportunity

SAP's discontinuation of IS-H (its healthcare-specific ERP module) directly impacts 60+ hospitals and clinics in the DACH region. SAP is transferring its hospital template to Snap Consulting, signaling a strategic pullback from managing healthcare-specific ERP as a core product. While this is most visible in DACH, the signal is global: SAP is repositioning away from deep healthcare vertical specificity toward generic S/4HANA migrations.

**Oracle's play**: Target SAP IS-H accounts proactively. Lead with the message that SAP is exiting healthcare-specific ERP; Oracle is doubling down with a purpose-built healthcare ERP suite that includes native EHR integration.

### 3.4 Infor/Lawson — End-of-Life Displacement Window

Infor Lawson has a confirmed 2030 end-of-life date. The 2026 RPI Consultants survey of the Infor community shows:
- Cloud migration activity has nearly doubled YOY
- 46% of Infor users now actively using AI (up from 20%)
- 42% still relying on manual test scripts — readiness gap signals urgency

Healthcare and public sector are Infor's primary verticals. These organizations are in active evaluation or early migration planning right now. The 4-year window before EOL means decisions need to be made in 2026-2027 to allow for proper implementation timelines.

### 3.5 PeopleSoft — Oracle's Own Installed Base

Oracle's PeopleSoft remains in active use across hundreds of health systems. While Oracle has continued to invest in PeopleSoft (Cloud Manager Image 21 released, continued GenAI AP automation features), the product is in "continuous modernization" mode — not greenfield development. Healthcare organizations using PeopleSoft for HR and financials represent the most cost-effective Oracle upgrade path. Recent case studies show healthcare PeopleSoft-to-Oracle-Fusion transitions being executed successfully (HTC Global Services case study) with minimal operational disruption.

### 3.6 Strata Decision Technology — Point Solution Threat in Finance

Strata Decision Technology's StrataJazz platform is widely deployed for hospital financial planning, budgeting, and performance analytics. Their data covers 650+ hospitals and 120+ health systems, representing approximately 25% of all U.S. provider spend. In February 2026, Strata reported health system margins negative to start 2026, with supply expenses up 12.3% YOY — data that drives urgency for ERP investment.

Strata is not a full ERP competitor but competes for the financial planning and analytics budget within finance committee discussions. Oracle's Financial Planning module and embedded analytics are the direct counter. The key differentiation: Strata is a point solution; Oracle provides the integrated operational + financial platform.

## 5. Strategic Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: Infor Lawson EOL Migration Wave (Highest Urgency — H1 2026)

**Size**: Infor has hundreds of healthcare customers on legacy Lawson. With 2030 EOL confirmed and cloud migration activity having nearly doubled in the past 12 months, the healthcare Lawson base is actively evaluating replace vs. migrate options.

**Window**: Decisions made in 2026-2027 will determine whether these accounts go to Infor CloudSuite or to a best-of-breed replacement. Oracle must insert itself into these evaluations now, before Infor CloudSuite implementations fully commence.

**Play**: Lead with the "Why restart your migration on an aging vendor when Oracle offers a single cloud suite including EHR?" narrative. Target CFOs and CIOs at health systems with >500 beds that are known Lawson customers. RPI Consultants' survey data showing 42% testing readiness gaps provides a compelling reason to consider a platform change vs. a risky Infor cloud migration.

**Target buyer**: CFOs (cost consolidation), CIOs (platform rationalization), CHROs (workforce management modernization).

### Opportunity 2: SAP IS-H Displacement — Healthcare-First Narrative (H1-H2 2026)

**Size**: SAP is actively retreating from healthcare-specific ERP. The IS-H discontinuation impacts DACH most directly but sends a global signal. North American SAP healthcare customers are now asking: "Is our SAP vendor still invested in healthcare?"

**Window**: Post-HIMSS (March 2026) through end of year. Oracle should be proactive — reach out to known SAP IS-H accounts and large SAP S/4HANA healthcare customers with the "SAP is leaving healthcare-specific ERP; Oracle is doubling down" message.

**Play**: Position Oracle as the only ERP vendor that has invested in a purpose-built healthcare ERP suite with native EHR, native SCM, and agentic AI — all in one cloud. Use the Oracle/CMS partnership to validate public-sector trust.

**Target buyer**: CIOs and IT leadership currently managing SAP footprints in health systems.

### Opportunity 3: PeopleSoft Healthcare Base Conversion — Upgrade Path Acceleration

**Size**: Hundreds of health systems remain on Oracle PeopleSoft for financials and HR. These are existing Oracle relationships — the competitive threat is lowest and the switching friction to Oracle Fusion Cloud is lower than any greenfield competitor.

**Window**: Ongoing, but 2026 urgency increases because Oracle's PeopleSoft investment narrative is shifting toward "continuous modernization" (not innovation). Smart customers recognize this as a graceful EOL signal. The window to convert PeopleSoft accounts to Oracle Cloud before they look at Workday or Infor as alternatives is narrowing.

**Play**: Run PeopleSoft-to-Oracle-Cloud upgrade campaigns targeting health systems with >3 years on PeopleSoft. Lead with GenAI AP automation ROI (up to 82% cost reduction in AP documented in case studies). Bundle HCM + ERP + SCM to maximize deal size and reduce competitive surface area.

**Target buyer**: CIOs already in the Oracle ecosystem, CFOs wanting to avoid rip-and-replace disruption.
