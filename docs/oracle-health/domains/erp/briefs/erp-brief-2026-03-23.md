## THE HEADLINE

Two legacy ERP platforms are burning — SAP is abandoning its healthcare-specific ERP module and Infor Lawson is four years from end-of-life — and Workday just extended its KLAS winning streak to nine years. The displacement window is open and Workday is racing to fill it before Oracle does.

---

## MARKET PULSE

- **$9 billion and growing at 7-8% annually.** The healthcare ERP TAM hit ~$9.01B in 2026 and is heading to $12-15B by 2031-2033. Finance/billing is still the primary entry point (30.4% of functional spend). North America owns 38.6% of that market. Hospitals = 53.8% of all ERP revenue. This is a large, growing, highly concentrated buyer market.

- **Health system margins just went negative.** Strata Decision Technology's February 2026 data shows health system median operating margin dropped to 1.3% (December 2025), with supply expenses up a staggering 12.3% YOY and drug costs up 6.1%. Margins went negative to start 2026. Every CFO conversation right now is about cost — which is an ERP conversation.

- **AI is no longer a pilot — it's a procurement criterion.** 80% of U.S. healthcare executives face board pressure for immediate AI ROI (KPMG). Within the Infor community alone, active AI adoption doubled from 20% to 46% in one year. Platforms that include agentic AI without an extra licensing fee now have a clear pricing advantage in procurement.

## COMPETITIVE ALERT

**Workday earned its 9th consecutive Best in KLAS for ERP for Large Organizations on February 4, 2026.** They were at HIMSS March 9-12 (booth #4208, Las Vegas) and ViVE in February aggressively positioning as the "operational backbone" for health systems. Their unified HR+Finance narrative is resonating.

Critical caveat: Workday's supply chain is structurally incomplete. They rely on Tecsys for warehouse management, Cargoson for transportation (which they don't even offer natively), and recently acquired Scout and Evisort for sourcing/contracts — neither described as "complete" in Oracle's February 2026 comparison. They charge extra for AI agents. Gartner's Source-to-Pay Magic Quadrant doesn't include Workday in the leadership tier. Oracle does.

SAP's IS-H healthcare solution is being discontinued, affecting 60+ hospitals and clinics in DACH. SAP is handing its hospital template off to Snap Consulting — a clear strategic retreat. North American SAP healthcare accounts are on notice that SAP is no longer committed to healthcare-specific ERP. This is a major displacement signal.

Infor Lawson: 2030 EOL is confirmed. Migration activity has nearly doubled. 42% of Infor healthcare customers still relying on manual test scripts — a readiness gap that makes the transition high-risk without a new platform partner.
