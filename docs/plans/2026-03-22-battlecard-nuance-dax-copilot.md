# BATTLECARD: Nuance / Microsoft DAX Copilot
**Updated**: 2026-03-22 | **Threat Level**: HIGH | **Installed Base**: ~400 health organizations

---

## QUICK DISMISS
"DAX Copilot is a documentation layer that bolts onto your EHR. We built clinical AI *inside* the EHR — documentation, coding, billing, decision support, one workflow. They solve the note. We solve the visit."

---

## WHEN WE WIN
- **Oracle Health EHR customers** — zero integration work, native data access, no middleware tax
- **Orgs that need more than documentation** — AI-assisted problem lists, order entry, and documentation-to-billing connectivity that DAX simply doesn't offer
- **Cost-conscious buyers** — $400-500/clinician/month vs. $600 DAX standard, with broader workflow coverage per dollar

## WHEN WE LOSE — BE HONEST
- **Multi-EHR environments** — Nuance is EHR-agnostic; if the org runs Epic + Cerner + MEDITECH, DAX covers all three and we only cover ours
- **Clinician brand loyalty** — physicians who already use Dragon/DAX daily resist switching; the "I love my Dragon" effect is real
- **Microsoft enterprise bundle deals** — when DAX gets wrapped into a broader Microsoft E5/Azure commitment, the incremental cost looks near-zero

## TOP 3 OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| *"We already have DAX and our docs love it."* | "They love the ambient capture — so do ours. The question is what happens *after* the note. DAX stops at documentation. For your Oracle Health environment, our AI reads and writes to the same database your clinicians use. DAX has to round-trip through APIs. Native isn't a limitation — it's the advantage." |

## LANDMINE QUESTIONS
Ask these early to reframe the conversation:

1. **"How much does your team spend annually maintaining the DAX-to-EHR integration?"** — Surfaces hidden cost of bolt-on architecture (typically $50-150K/year in IT overhead)
2. **"Does DAX connect your documentation directly to your coding and billing workflows?"** — Answer is no. Opens the full-workflow conversation.
3. **"When DAX generates a note, can it also update the problem list and suggest orders in context?"** — Answer is no. Highlights documentation-only limitation.
