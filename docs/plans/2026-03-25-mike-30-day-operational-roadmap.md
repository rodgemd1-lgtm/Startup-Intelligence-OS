## Battlecard & Segment Profile Replication Process

### The Repeatable Pipeline (per artifact)

```
PHASE 1: INTAKE & SCOPING (Day 1)
├── Identify target competitor or segment
├── Define audience (Sales, Strategist, Exec)
├── Set output format (battlecard template / profile template)
└── Assign lead strategist (Hub-and-Spoke: strategist leads, M&CI supports)

PHASE 2: AUTOMATED RESEARCH ASSEMBLY (Days 1-2)
├── Ellen pulls from multi-source intelligence:
│   ├── KLAS reports and ratings
│   ├── DHC (Digital Health Connect) data
│   ├── CMS / regulatory filings
│   ├── SEC filings (public companies)
│   ├── Conference intelligence (recent events)
│   ├── Brave/Exa/Firecrawl web scraping
│   └── GLG / expert network insights (when available)
├── Ellen assembles draft with source citations
├── ~65% of content auto-populated (target state)
└── Source verification protocol applied (100% verified)

PHASE 3: STRATEGIST REVIEW & ENRICHMENT (Days 2-3)
├── Assigned strategist reviews Ellen's draft
├── Adds domain expertise, customer context, deal intelligence
├── Validates competitive claims against known deals
├── Fills gaps Ellen couldn't cover (internal tribal knowledge)
└── Hub-and-Spoke: strategist owns this, M&CI consults

PHASE 4: QUALITY GATE & APPROVAL (Day 3-4)
├── M&CI reviews for accuracy, completeness, messaging
├── Cross-reference with existing battlecards for consistency
├── Sales/GTM spot-check (does this match what they see in deals?)
└── Final sign-off

PHASE 5: DISTRIBUTION & FEEDBACK (Day 4-5)
├── Publish to SharePoint knowledge base
├── Push to Sales via existing channels
├── Track usage and feedback
└── Schedule refresh cycle (quarterly or trigger-based)
```

### Scaling the Process
- **Week 1-2**: Mike runs first 2 battlecards end-to-end using this process
- **Week 3-4**: Onboarded strategists (Catherine, Rosen) run their first battlecard with M&CI support
- **Month 2+**: Strategists run independently; M&CI shifts to quality gate and complex requests
- **Target cadence**: 1 battlecard per strategist per month = 6/month at full scale

### What Makes It Repeatable
1. **Ellen does the heavy lifting** — 65% auto-assembled means strategists start from a draft, not a blank page
2. **Template-driven** — same format every time, same sections, same quality bar
3. **Source verification built in** — no artifact ships without 100% verified sources
4. **Hub-and-Spoke model** — strategists own production, M&CI owns quality and complex research
5. **SharePoint as the single distribution point** — one place to publish, one place to find

## Sales Enablement Go-to-Market Strategy

### Who to Go to First
1. **GTM team (first priority)** — They're closest to active deals. Competitive positioning directly impacts win rates. They'll use artifacts immediately and provide fast feedback.
2. **Sales leadership** — After GTM proves the model, bring Sales leaders in to see results. They become champions who pull enablement into their teams.
3. **Marketing** — Once we have 2-3 proven artifacts, partner with Marketing to scale distribution and create polished versions.

### How to Actually Start
**Step 1: Embed in 2-3 live deal cycles (Week 3-4)**
- Ask GTM: "What are your 3 biggest competitive deals right now?"
- Build deal-specific competitive positioning packages for those deals
- Deliver in the format they already use (not a new format they have to learn)

**Step 2: Package learnings into reusable artifacts (Week 4-5)**
- Take the deal-specific work and generalize it
- Create: competitive positioning template, deal prep checklist, demo intelligence brief
- These become the first Sales Enablement artifacts for the KPI

**Step 3: Build the feedback loop (Ongoing)**
- After each deal, capture: what worked, what didn't, what was missing
- Feed insights back into Ellen's knowledge base
- Each deal makes the next artifact better

### How to Get Traction and Get Ahead of It
1. **Solve an active pain point** — Don't build materials hoping someone uses them. Find the pain, solve it, then replicate.
2. **Pricing and packaging intelligence** — This is a high-value, underserved area. Start building competitive pricing comparisons that inform commercial strategy.
3. **Amol as the point person** — Get Amol on GLG expert networks producing sales-facing battlecards. He becomes the Sales team's go-to intelligence resource.
4. **Weekly "competitive flash"** — Short, punchy competitive updates pushed to Sales/GTM. Low effort, high visibility. Gets the team used to consuming M&CI output.
5. **Measure and report** — Track which artifacts get used, which deals they touch. Report to Matt quarterly: "M&CI sourced insights cited in X deals."
