# BATTLECARD: MEDITECH
**Updated**: 2026-03-22 | **Threat Level**: MEDIUM | **Market Share**: 14.8% acute care

---

## QUICK DISMISS
MEDITECH is a solid community hospital EHR that hits a ceiling the moment a health system needs enterprise scale, AI-driven workflows, or anything beyond acute care.

## WHEN WE WIN
- Health systems planning growth beyond 400 beds or multi-entity expansion
- Organizations prioritizing AI-embedded clinical workflows and autonomous agents
- Buyers who need EHR + ERP on one platform without bolting on third-party systems
- MEDITECH legacy shops (Magic/Client/Server) facing an 18-24 month migration to Expanse anyway

## WHEN WE LOSE — BE HONEST
- Small independent community hospitals (<250 beds) where MEDITECH's KLAS reputation and relationships run deep
- Budget-constrained buyers seduced by MaaS subscription pricing who aren't doing a 5-year TCO analysis
- Organizations where "good enough" wins and there's no appetite for transformation

## LANDMINE QUESTIONS
- "How will your EHR handle our growth if we acquire two more hospitals in the next 3 years?"
- "Can you show us AI that's already reducing documentation burden with published clinical evidence?"
- "Walk us through your interoperability roadmap — TEFCA, QHIN, bidirectional FHIR exchange."

## PROOF POINTS
- **DoD MHS GENESIS**: 197,000+ users, 3,890 locations — the largest single EHR deployment in the world
- **AI Clinical Impact**: Yale/JAMA studies — 20-30% reduction in documentation time, 74% lower odds of burnout
- **Interoperability**: Active TEFCA QHIN application; FHIR-native architecture

## PRICING INTEL
MEDITECH MaaS subscription looks attractive upfront (~130+ deployments across 38 states), but bundles Zynx/IMO to mask per-module costs. Always request a 5-year TCO comparison including ERP, interoperability middleware, and analytics — Oracle's unified platform typically closes the gap or wins outright.
