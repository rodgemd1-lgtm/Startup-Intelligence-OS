## QUICK DISMISS
"Epic gives you a bigger EHR — Oracle gives you an intelligent health platform. Epic declined to share new contract data with KLAS for the first time ever, and that should tell you something about where their momentum is heading."

## WHEN WE WIN
- **Health systems tired of vendor lock-in** that want best-of-breed AI/analytics without being trapped in a closed ecosystem
- **Organizations needing enterprise integration** — Oracle Fusion ERP (finance, SCM, HCM) natively connected to the clinical record is something Epic cannot replicate
- **Federal/large-scale deployments** where proven scale matters — 197,200 users across 3,890 DoD locations is the largest single EHR deployment on earth
- **Cost-conscious buyers** doing a 10-year TCO analysis who balk at Epic's $150M-$500M+ implementation price tag

## WHEN WE LOSE — BE HONEST
- **Large academic medical centers** already deep in Epic's ecosystem with heavy Community Connect networks — the switching cost math rarely works in our favor
- **Buyers who weight KLAS satisfaction above all else** — Epic has won Best in KLAS for large hospitals 11 consecutive years, and our partner confidence score dropped to 47%
- **Deals where the CIO's peers are all on Epic** — their installed base creates referral network effects we can't match today
- **Organizations that lost confidence during our Cerner transition** — 57 unique acute care customer losses (2022-2024) gave Epic a compelling "safe choice" narrative
