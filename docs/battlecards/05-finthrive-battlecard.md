# BATTLECARD: FinThrive
**Updated**: 2026-03-22 | **Threat Level**: HIGH | **Penetration**: 3 of 5 U.S. hospitals

---

## QUICK DISMISS
"FinThrive is a billing cleanup layer that sits outside the EHR. We prevent the billing problems they charge you to fix. They're the best band-aid in the market — but we eliminate the wound."

## WHEN WE WIN
- **Prospect already runs Oracle Health EHR** — native integration crushes bolt-on middleware every time
- **CFO AND CMO are both in the room** — our clinical-to-financial story is unmatched; FinThrive only speaks CFO
- **CMS-0057-F compliance is urgent** — we ship it native; they have to build connectors to your EHR

## WHEN WE LOSE — BE HONEST
- **Epic or MEDITECH shops** — FinThrive is EHR-agnostic and already embedded; we have no EHR leverage here
- **CFO-only decision with no clinical stakeholder** — FinThrive's RCM depth and 60% installed base make them the safe pick
- **"We just need better billing"** — if the prospect doesn't value upstream clinical optimization, our differentiator doesn't land

## LANDMINE QUESTIONS
- "Can you show us how Fusion handles a clinical documentation gap that causes a denial — not just flags it after the fact, but prevents it?"
- "How many of your Fusion customers are fully live on the unified platform vs. still running legacy modules?"
- "When CMS-0057-F enforcement hits, does Fusion generate compliant data natively or does it depend on an EHR feed you don't control?"

## PROOF POINTS
- **St. Joseph's Health**: $39.6M ROI with Oracle's autonomous reimbursement platform — clinical and financial on one system

FinThrive prices per-claim or as % of net revenue recovered — looks cheap until you realize you're paying them to fix problems that shouldn't exist. Oracle bundles RCM into EHR platform deals; position as "you're already paying for this" vs. FinThrive's additive cost layer.
