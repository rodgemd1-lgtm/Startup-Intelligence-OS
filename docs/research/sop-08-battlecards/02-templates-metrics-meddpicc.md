Objection Handling Frameworks\n\n#### LAER (Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond)\n**Origin:** Challenger/RAIN Group practitioner community. Widely used in B2B SaaS and enterprise sales.\n\n**Protocol:**\n1. **Listen** — Hear the full objection. Let 2 seconds pass after they finish. Do not interrupt or pre-answer.\n2. **Acknowledge** — Repeat the concern back in your own words. Signals active listening.\n3. **Explore** — Ask a clarifying question to surface the real objection beneath the stated one. (\"Help me understand what's driving that concern...\")\n4. **Respond** — Deliver the response: corrective information, reframe, or next step.\n\n**When to use:** Complex objections where the stated concern may not be the real concern (common in health IT: \"your support is bad\" may actually be \"we heard from a peer institution that they had a bad implementation\"). LAER creates space to surface the real issue before responding.\n\n#### Feel, Felt, Found (3F)\n**Origin:** Classic sales methodology, widely attributed to early consultative selling tradition.\n\n**Protocol:**\n- **Feel:** \"I understand how you feel...\" (validate the concern)\n- **Felt:** \"Others in similar situations have felt the same way...\" (normalize, reduce isolation)\n- **Found:** \"What they found was...\" (deliver the counter-evidence or resolution)\n\n**When to use:** Emotionally charged objections (reputation-based, past bad experience). Strong for situations where a health system has heard peer institution negative stories about Oracle Health — 3F allows empathy before pivoting to updated evidence.\n\n#### Challenger / Reframe\n**Origin:** Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson, 2011).
