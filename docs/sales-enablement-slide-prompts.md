### SOP-09: Win/Loss Analysis

**Slide 1 — What It Is**
```
Title: Win/Loss Analysis — Why We Really Win and Lose Deals
Subtitle: SOP-09 | P1 — #1 Most Impactful CI Program (Industry Research)

Content:
- Behavioral science-informed interview methodology
- Penetrates beneath stated reasons to uncover actual decision drivers
- The 44-Point Price Gap: buyers cite price 62% of the time, but it's the actual
  driver only 18% of the time
- Stated reason matches actual driver only 36% of the time

Key stats:
- Companies with win/loss programs see 15-30% revenue increases (Gartner)
- Win/loss + CI combined = 3x more likely to report significant impact (Klue 2025)
- 85% of CRM closed-lost data is fundamentally wrong (Corporate Visions)
```

**Slide 2 — How It Works**
```
Title: Behavioral Interview Methodology

Process:
1. Deal Selection — stratified sampling (not just big/memorable deals)
2. Interview Design — cognitive interview techniques, not satisfaction surveys
3. Execution — 45-60 min structured interviews, 2-3 weeks post-decision
4. Analysis — pattern coding across multiple interviews, not anecdotal
5. Predictive Modeling — Monte Carlo deal prediction from interview data
6. Distribution — findings routed to Sales, Product, Marketing, Exec

Key insight: Traditional win/loss asks "why did you choose us?" and takes
the answer at face value. This methodology probes beneath surface answers
to find the actual decision architecture.
```

**Slide 3 — What You Get**
```
Title: Win/Loss Outputs & Impact

Deliverables:
- Quarterly Win/Loss Report with pattern analysis
- Deal Decision Driver Dashboard (actual vs stated reasons)
- Competitive Positioning Recommendations (by competitor)
- Product Feedback Loop (validated customer voice into roadmap)
- Predictive Deal Scoring (Monte Carlo model)

Impact potential:
- 15-30% revenue increase from program insights
- Up to 50% improvement in win rates
- Validated competitive positioning vs assumption-based
- CRM data quality improvement (replacing garbage closed-lost data)
```

### SOP-10: Pricing & Packaging Intelligence

**Slide 1 — What It Is**
```
Title: Pricing & Packaging Intelligence — Win Deals on Structure, Not Discounts
Subtitle: SOP-10 | P1 — Win Rate Driver, High Sales Value

Content:
- End-to-end pricing intelligence program for Oracle Health
- How pricing data is collected, validated, confidence-coded, and published
- Covers: competitive pricing comparisons, discount band modeling, packaging analysis
- Two analytical models: PGPS (Pricing Gap Priority Score) + Monte Carlo Discount Band

Why it matters:
- When a rep knows Epic's RCM module discounts 20-28% on competitive bids,
  they stop leaving money on the table
- When they know Cerner pricing crosses a threshold at 500 beds, they can
  architect proposals that are structurally cheaper at that deal size
```

**Slide 2 — How It Works**
```
Title: Pricing Data Lifecycle

Sources:
- Win/Loss interviews (highest fidelity)
- Deal Desk data (actual negotiated prices)
- GLG / expert network calls
- SEC filings (public companies)
- Industry reports (KLAS, Gartner)
- Conference intelligence

Confidence scoring: Every data point tagged HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW / UNVERIFIED
Validation protocol: cross-reference minimum 2 independent sources

Analytical models:
- PGPS Algorithm: prioritizes which pricing gaps matter most for win rate
- Monte Carlo Discount Band: simulates expected discount ranges by deal size,
  competitor, segment
```

**Slide 3 — What You Get**
```
Title: Pricing Intelligence Outputs

Deliverables:
- Master Pricing Workbook (Excel, maintained quarterly)

- Competitive discount band analysis
- Packaging comparison matrices
- Pricing trend alerts (event-driven)

KPIs:
- Pricing data coverage: % of active competitors with validated pricing intel
- Deal influence: # deals where pricing intel was cited as factor
- Accuracy: post-deal validation of pricing predictions
- Freshness: quarterly update cycle, event-driven triggers
```

### SOP-12: Competitive Response Playbook

**Slide 1 — What It Is**
```
Title: Competitive Response Playbook — From Detection to Field Response in Hours
Subtitle: SOP-12 | P2 — Structured Response to Competitor Moves

Content:
- Formalized process for detecting, assessing, and responding to competitive events
- Replaces ad hoc response with tier-based playbook
- Covers: product launches, pricing changes, acquisitions, executive moves,
  win/loss events, market events, narrative events

Key stat: 24-hour response vs 1-week response to a major competitive move
is worth $2.1M-$6.8M in protected pipeline per event
```

**Slide 2 — How It Works**
```
Title: 6-Phase Response Protocol

Phase 1: Detection & Triage — signal monitoring, severity classification
Phase 2: Impact Assessment — pipeline exposure, deal risk scoring
Phase 3: Stakeholder Notification — tiered notification by severity
Phase 4: Response Recommendation — positioning guidance, talking points
Phase 5: Battlecard Update — rapid refresh of affected battlecards
Phase 6: Deal Protection — active deal intervention where needed

Response tiers:
- Tier 1 (Critical): Full response in 4 hours — executive notification + field guidance
- Tier 2 (High): Response in 24 hours — battlecard update + field alert
- Tier 3 (Medium): Response in 48-72 hours — standard update cycle
- Tier 4 (Low): Next scheduled update — monitor only
```

**Slide 3 — What You Get**
```
Title: Response Playbook Outputs & Measurement

Deliverables per event:
- Competitive Event Brief (what happened, what it means)
- Field Talking Points (what to say when asked)
- Battlecard Update (if applicable)
- Deal Protection Package (for at-risk pipeline)
- Post-Response Review (what worked, what didn't)

KPIs:
- Mean time to field response (by tier)
- Pipeline protected ($) per competitive event
- Competitive Event Impact Score (CEIS) — predictive algorithm
- Field satisfaction with response quality and speed
```
