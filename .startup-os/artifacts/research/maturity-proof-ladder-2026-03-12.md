# Maturity Proof Ladder

**Research question:** What parts of Startup Intelligence OS maturity can be closed by tests, simulations, Monte Carlo analysis, and dataset ingestion, and what parts still require live operating proof?

## Definitions
- **Data maturity:** breadth, freshness, and relevance of source coverage.
- **Capability maturity:** whether the OS has the required artifacts, protocols, agents, and flows.
- **Simulated maturity:** how well the system performs under tests, benchmarks, synthetic scenarios, evals, and modeled operating conditions.
- **Operational maturity:** how well the system performs in repeated live use with humans, decisions, outputs, and outcome reviews.

## Methods
- unit and integration tests for reliability
- eval suites for output quality
- scenario runs for routing and decision quality
- Monte Carlo analysis for forecast and risk-sensitive domains
- benchmark libraries and dataset ingestion for evidence density
- live run history and outcome reviews for final operating proof

## Benchmark targets

### What tests and simulations can legitimately prove
- `10/10` data coverage for a department or section
- `10/10` capability coverage for a department or section
- stable routing behavior
- prompt or artifact quality against a rubric
- forecast robustness under defined assumptions
- failure modes across scenario sets

### What they cannot fully prove alone
- consistent human adoption
- facilitator quality in live sessions
- production decision quality over time
- organizational trust in outputs
- durable business results

## Monte Carlo applicability

### High leverage
- Founder Decision Room: option downside, scenario comparison, reversal thresholds
- Revenue & Growth Studio: funnel conversion ranges, CAC/payback, forecast bands
- Finance & Operating Cadence Studio: runway, variance, spend sensitivity
- Data & Decision Science Studio: forecast intervals, experiment uncertainty

### Moderate leverage
- Product & Experience Studio: effort/value scenario tradeoffs, rollout risk
- Engineering & Agent Systems Studio: reliability and failure budget simulations

### Lower leverage
- Job Studio: useful for planning throughput and asset readiness, not for proving learner impact alone
- Consumer User Studio and Marketing & Narrative Studio: simulations help prioritization, but human evaluation is still decisive
- Talent & Org Design Studio: scenario analysis helps headcount planning, but role quality still needs live proof

## Synthesis
Yes, we can move much closer to 10 by adding more tests, evals, simulations, Monte Carlo runs, and curated datasets. That is the right way to close the remaining **simulated maturity** gap.

No, we cannot honestly use those alone to claim **operational 10 out of 10** across the whole OS. The final step requires live repeated proof:
- completed runs
- outcome reviews
- rubric-backed evaluations
- time-based consistency
- decision or business impact

## Practical rule
- Use datasets to close evidence and benchmark gaps.
- Use tests and evals to close correctness and output-quality gaps.
- Use Monte Carlo and scenario simulation to close forecast and risk-analysis gaps.
- Use live runs and outcome reviews to close the final maturity gap.

## Unknowns
- How many live runs per department should be required before a 10 out of 10 claim is credible?
- Which departments need human-in-the-loop reviews versus autonomous pass criteria?
- How much live evidence is enough for Job Studio training quality versus founder decision quality?

## Recommended threshold model
- **Up to 8 or 9:** can be reached with strong datasets, evals, simulations, and repeated synthetic runs.
- **10:** requires live operating proof with repeated success, writeback, and demonstrated outcome quality.
