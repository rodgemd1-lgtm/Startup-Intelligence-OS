# Startup Intelligence OS Zero-to-Ten Maturity System

**Date:** 2026-03-12  
**Scale:** `0 = completely immature`, `10 = best in the world`  
**Purpose:** Define what full maturity means across all core departments, studios, features, functions, tools, and operating layers.

## Objective
Replace vague maturity language with one shared operating system for scoring, roadmap decisions, build sequencing, and operator visibility.

## Universal Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 | Missing. No defined surface, no owner, no process, no data, no artifact path. |
| 1 | Named but not operational. Exists as an idea, stub, or isolated file. |
| 2 | Ad hoc. Can be used manually by the founder or operator, but not reliably repeated. |
| 3 | Emerging. Reusable in one narrow flow with explicit artifacts and partial data. |
| 4 | Functional. One operator can run it end to end with acceptable quality most of the time. |
| 5 | Team-ready. A second operator can run it with the same outputs using clear playbooks and evidence. |
| 6 | Department-grade. Metrics, cadences, writeback, and stage gates are live. |
| 7 | Cross-company reusable. The system can be inherited and adapted without rebuilding from scratch. |
| 8 | Self-improving. Evaluation, memory, data refresh, and failure-pattern loops change future behavior. |
| 9 | Market-leading. The department or system is unusually strong, fast, grounded, and hard to replicate. |
| 10 | Best in the world. The surface is category-defining in speed, rigor, compound learning, data leverage, and operator usability. |

## What "10" Means Across Every System Layer

### 1. Company Genome
At `10`, every company, project, department, studio, capability, decision, artifact, run, experiment, and assumption has explicit ownership, lifecycle, dependencies, and handoff rules. There is no orphan work, no duplicate authority, and no ambiguity about where to start or where output should land.

### 2. Evidence Graph
At `10`, every major recommendation is grounded in named, queryable evidence with provenance, freshness, confidence, and reusable source maps. Raw corpora, extracted corpora, repo harvests, screenshots, evaluation transcripts, and local working memory all resolve into one governed graph.

### 3. Cognitive Studios
At `10`, every department can perceive, diagnose, generate options, critique, simulate, produce, and write back. Studios do not only answer; they reason, challenge, and improve.

### 4. Trust and Governance
At `10`, privacy, security, accessibility, claims review, and approval traces are inherited by default. The system can move fast without becoming reckless.

### 5. Operator Console
At `10`, Jake's control plane shows maturity, signals, routes, blockers, next-best actions, and readiness visually. The operator does not hunt for status across files.

### 6. Memory and Evaluation
At `10`, every run changes the future system. Postmortems, rubrics, transcript reviews, experiment outcomes, and asset reuse are automatic and visible.

## Full-Maturity Criteria By Functional Layer

### Data Acquisition and Scraping
At `10`:
- All available scraping tools are live, key-connected, monitored, and resumable.
- Firecrawl, Playwright, Jina, Git harvesting, local binary extraction, and storage mirroring are orchestrated through manifests and retry-safe jobs.
- Raw data volume is large enough to matter: tens of gigabytes across company, domain, training, and evaluation corpora.
- Every harvest produces a manifest, summary, failure log, freshness stamp, and ingestion path.

### Binary Extraction and Normalization
At `10`:
- `.docx`, `.doc`, `.pptx`, `.pdf`, `.xlsx`, `.xlsm`, `.xlsb`, `.eml`, `.msg`, `.csv`, and text-like repo files are normalized into markdown or text artifacts automatically.
- Extraction keeps sheet names, slide grouping, document titles, and source paths.
- Failures are isolated and recoverable; unsupported formats are visible, not silent.

### Persona and Agent Access
At `10`:
- Every persona, routed agent, and department can access the same current corpus through governed data types and context bundles.
- Personal memory, company delivery memory, training materials, evaluation data, and open research are separated by namespace but callable together when appropriate.
- Every action packet lists the context sources that must be attached before work begins.

### Protocols and Processes
At `10`:
- Every department has intake, diagnosis, asset production, evaluation, writeback, and escalation protocols.
- Every repeated workflow has a playbook, rubric, and stage gate.
- No high-value process depends on tacit operator memory alone.

### Tools
At `10`, each tool category is complete:
- **Acquisition tools:** scrape, mirror, clone, crawl, refresh, resume.
- **Corpus tools:** extract, tag, dedupe, summarize, locate, ingest.
- **Decision tools:** route, compare options, create decisions, sequence work.
- **Training tools:** generate decks, guides, handouts, activities, examples, rubrics.
- **Evaluation tools:** transcript scoring, quality review, confidence scoring, failure library.
- **Operator tools:** dashboards, scorecards, signals, graph views, next-best actions.

## Department "10" Definitions

### Founder Decision Room
At `10`, it frames the right decision before work starts, compares credible options, attaches evidence and risks, routes execution, and closes the loop with outcome tracking.

### Consumer User Studio
At `10`, it continuously turns user signal into ranked opportunities, validated assumptions, experiments, and reusable evidence for every downstream team.

### Product & Experience Studio
At `10`, it can move from problem framing to implementation-ready workflow design with critique, instrumentation, and evaluation built in.

### Marketing & Narrative Studio
At `10`, it produces proof-backed messaging systems, decks, memos, articles, and campaign assets that all descend from one research spine.

### Engineering & Agent Systems Studio
At `10`, it safely upgrades the OS, the dashboard, the Susan runtime, and the data pipelines with tests, monitoring, and reuse leverage.

### Job Studio
At `10`, it is a fully reusable training, memory, opportunity, and personal operator department with giant corpora, strong evaluation, and repeatable output systems.

### Data & Decision Science Studio
At `10`, it transforms raw activity, evidence, and evaluation data into scorecards, simulations, forecasts, and decision-quality support.

### Revenue & Growth Studio
At `10`, it maps ICPs, proof, channels, offers, and conversion systems into one measurable growth engine.

### Finance & Operating Cadence Studio
At `10`, it keeps the company legible through KPI trees, operating reviews, resource calls, and variance response.

### Talent & Org Design Studio
At `10`, it turns capability gaps into org blueprints, role scorecards, hiring loops, onboarding, and readiness tracking.

### Trust & Governance Studio
At `10`, it makes trust a system default through clear gates, audits, remediation loops, and release discipline.

## Job Studio Training Factory "10" Definition

At `10`, the training factory:
- maintains gigabytes of grounded training, behavioral-science, evaluation, and workflow data
- can build an eight-session program from a reusable asset system
- produces decks, guides, handouts, activities, transcripts, and rubrics automatically
- scores sessions and conversations for transfer, confidence, and usefulness
- improves future training from review outcomes
- supports AI foundations, Ellen enablement, market intelligence, and new domain tracks without rebuilding the stack
