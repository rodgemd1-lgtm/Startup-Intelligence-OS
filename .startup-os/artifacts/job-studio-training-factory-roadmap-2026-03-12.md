# Job Studio Training Factory Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-03-12  
**Priority:** Urgent  
**Department:** Job Studio  
**Use case:** Immediate Oracle Health strategist enablement and long-range department buildout

## Objective
Build Job Studio as a sixth core department with a training factory that can bring strategists up to speed on AI, transition them into Ellen in Gen Chat, and produce the decks, handouts, facilitation guides, activities, and evaluation assets needed to run the program well.

## Framing
The fastest correct move is to treat Job Studio as both:
- a personal execution and memory department
- a training and enablement department with reusable operator assets

The eight-session training arc should be structured in two layers:
- **Track C, Sessions 1-3:** AI foundations for the strategist
- **Track B, Sessions 4-8:** Ellen in Gen Chat for Oracle Health workflows

The previous marketing intelligence and competitive intelligence requirement remains critical, but it now becomes the main applied scenario set inside Sessions 4-8 rather than a separate standalone arc.

## Recommendation
Stand up Job Studio as a sixth core department now, and use the Oracle Health enablement repo plus the attached Oracle corpus as the seed system for the training factory.

## Eight-Session Arc

### Track C: Strategist AI Foundations

#### Session 1: What AI Is and How to Work With It
- Objective: Give the strategist a useful mental model of AI, its strengths, its failure modes, and when not to trust first-pass output.
- Required assets: foundation deck, one-page handout, prompt anatomy worksheet, bad-vs-good example set.
- Activity: compare a vague prompt against a scoped prompt and diagnose what changed.

#### Session 2: Workflow Discipline, Prompting, and Evidence
- Objective: Teach structured prompting, source discipline, confidence calibration, and how to move from question to workflow.
- Required assets: workflow deck, handout, exercise prompts, evaluator rubric.
- Activity: run I-01 and U-01 on a live strategic request.

#### Session 3: Transition From General AI Use to Ellen-Led Work
- Objective: Teach when to stop using raw chat as a general assistant and when to transition into an orchestrated Ellen workflow.
- Required assets: transition deck, decision tree handout, role map, orchestration examples.
- Activity: classify real strategist tasks into direct AI, Ellen workflow, or human-only work.

### Track B: Ellen in Gen Chat for Oracle Health

#### Session 4: Ellen Orientation and System Navigation
- Objective: Explain Ellen's role, slash-command structure, piece selection, and how the Oracle Health system is organized.
- Required assets: Ellen system map, prompt card set, live demo script, learner cheat sheet.
- Activity: take one Oracle Health ask and route it to the correct piece.

#### Session 5: Marketing Intelligence and Competitive Intelligence With Ellen
- Objective: Teach how Ellen supports landscape scanning, competitor profiling, messaging proof, and executive-grade strategic outputs.
- Required assets: workflow deck, competitor brief template, source checklist, worked example library.
- Activity: build and critique a competitive landscape brief for an Oracle Health topic.

#### Session 6: Source Verification, Anti-Hallucination, and Confidence
- Objective: Make source verification and confidence scoring a reflex inside the Ellen workflow.
- Required assets: quality gate deck, verification handout, failure-pattern transcript set, audit rubric.
- Activity: red-team one AI-generated brief and score it.

#### Session 7: Deliverable Assembly in Gen Chat
- Objective: Show how Ellen transitions from research and analysis into memos, slide narratives, handouts, and training outputs.
- Required assets: deliverable assembly deck, output templates, asset cascade handout, trainer notes.
- Activity: assemble a slide narrative and executive memo from one proof spine.

#### Session 8: Capstone End-to-End Run
- Objective: Run one full Oracle Health strategist workflow from intake to verified deliverable.
- Required assets: capstone packet, scoring rubric, panel review sheet, reflection form.
- Activity: complete a full team simulation and debrief against the scoring rubric.

## Full Development Process
1. Intake the training objective, audience, and delivery horizon.
2. Harvest evidence from the Oracle enablement repo, the Oracle Health corpus, and Job Studio memory.
3. Define session learning objectives, common failure modes, and transfer goals.
4. Produce the asset bundle for each session:
   - deck
   - facilitator guide
   - learner handout
   - activity script
   - named examples
   - evaluation rubric
5. Pilot the session with an explicit review panel.
6. Write back the learning results, transcript failures, and revised best practices into Job Studio artifacts and skills.

## Full Maturity Roadmap

### 0-30 days
- Finish Track C and Track B session maps.
- Create one operator packet for all eight sessions.
- Produce the current-state visual and scoring rubric.
- Create the first named example library from Oracle markdown assets and the prompt library.

### 30-90 days
- Convert the highest-value Oracle `.docx`, `.pptx`, `.pdf`, and `.xlsx` files into reusable training references.
- Stand up the evaluation panel with conversational quality and trainer scoring.
- Package the first Job Studio skill set for Codex.

### 90-180 days
- Add reusable asset templates for decks, handouts, activities, and facilitator scripts.
- Add current-state and readiness views to the operator console.
- Connect training writeback to department and capability maturity deltas.

### 6-24 months
- Reuse Job Studio as a full training and enablement department across companies and cohorts.
- Build deep corpus operations, reusable eval sets, and multi-mode delivery.
- Make Job Studio a reference department for training, memory, opportunity support, and AI workflow enablement.

## Risks
- The Oracle corpus is large, but much of it is still binary and not yet available as structured reusable examples.
- Without evaluation gates, the training may look polished but fail to transfer behavior.
- Without explicit asset production rules, the team may recreate materials instead of compounding them.
