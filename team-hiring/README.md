# Team & Hiring — Building an AI-First Startup Team

> Hiring strategy, compensation, culture, and remote team management from 0 → $10M.

---

## 1. Hiring Roadmap by Stage

### Phase 1: Founding Team (0-3 people)
- **Critical roles**: Technical co-founder + business co-founder
- **Key**: Complementary skills, shared vision, high trust
- **Equity**: 40-60% split with 4yr vesting / 1yr cliff

### Phase 2: First 5 Hires ($0-$500K ARR)
| Order | Role | Why |
|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Full-stack engineer | Ship product faster |
| 2 | Designer (product/UX) | Differentiate with great UX |
| 3 | Growth/marketing generalist | Drive acquisition |
| 4 | Customer success / support | Retain first customers |
| 5 | 2nd engineer | Scale development capacity |

### Phase 3: Building the Team ($500K-$2M ARR)
- Engineering: 3-5 engineers (specialize: frontend, backend, ML)
- Product: 1 product manager
- Sales: 1 AE or SDR (if sales-assisted)
- Marketing: Content writer, demand gen
- CS: 1-2 support/success reps

### Phase 4: Scaling ($2M-$10M ARR)
- Engineering: 8-15 engineers, 1-2 eng managers
- Product: 2-3 PMs
- Design: 2-3 designers
- Sales: 3-5 AEs, 2-3 SDRs, sales manager
- Marketing: Team of 3-5 specialists
- CS: 3-5 reps, CS manager
- Operations: 1 ops lead, 1 finance/admin

## 2. AI-First Hiring Strategy

### The AI Multiplier
In 2026, AI-augmented teams are 3-5x more productive. Hire for:
- **AI fluency**: Every team member should use Claude/AI tools daily
- **Fewer, better people**: 5 AI-powered engineers > 15 traditional
- **Full-stack generalists**: People who can do more with AI assistance
- **Learning velocity**: How fast they adopt new tools

### Interview for AI Fluency
1. "Walk me through how you'd use AI to solve [problem]"
2. "Show me something you built with AI assistance"
3. "How do you validate AI-generated code?"
4. Give a take-home that explicitly allows AI tools

## 4. Hiring Process

### Efficient Hiring Pipeline
1. **Source** (1-2 weeks): Job post + referrals + outbound
2. **Screen** (30 min): Culture fit + basic qualifications
3. **Technical** (60-90 min): Practical exercise, not leetcode
4. **Team fit** (45 min): Work with 2-3 team members
5. **Offer** (1-2 days): Move fast for top candidates

### Where to Source
| Channel | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| **Referrals** | Highest quality, fastest |
| **Twitter/X** | Engineering, AI talent |
| **LinkedIn** | All roles, outbound |
| **AngelList/Wellfound** | Startup-oriented talent |
| **Hacker News: Who's Hiring** | Engineering |
| **RemoteOK** / **WeWorkRemotely** | Remote talent |
| **Toptal** | Vetted freelancers |
| **Arc.dev** | Remote developers |

### Technical Assessment (Skip Leetcode)
- **Paid take-home project** (4-6 hours, relevant to actual work)
- **Pair programming session** (45 min, problem-solving together)
- **Code review exercise** (review real PR, discuss tradeoffs)
- **System design** (whiteboard architecture for relevant problem)

## 5. Remote Team Culture

### Async-First Communication
| Principle | Practice |
|-----------|----------|
| **Write it down** | All decisions documented in Notion |
| **Default async** | Slack messages, Loom videos, PRs |
| **Meetings are last resort** | Only when async fails |
| **Time zone respect** | Core hours overlap of 4 hours |
| **Documentation over meetings** | RFC documents for big decisions |

### Culture Building (Remote)
- Weekly all-hands (30 min, video on)
- Monthly team social (game, virtual event)
- Quarterly off-site (if budget allows)
- Pair programming / buddy system for new hires
- Public kudos channel in Slack
- Learning budget ($500-$1,000/yr per person)
