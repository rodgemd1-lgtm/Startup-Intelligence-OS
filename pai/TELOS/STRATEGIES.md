# Strategies

## Business Strategy

### Cross-Portfolio Synergy
Run 3 companies that share infrastructure. What Susan learns for Oracle Health feeds Alex Recruiting and vice versa. The OS is the product AND the operations layer.

### Dogfood Then Productize
Build Jake/Susan for Mike first. Once it works at Mani-level operations, package it for other founders. Revenue follows proof.

### Mani-Level Operations
Named after the goal: operate like Mani (Google's internal AI ops benchmark). Routine decisions automated, exceptions surfaced, human in the loop only for high-leverage calls.

## Technical Strategy

### Miessler Architecture
Follow Daniel Miessler's PAI v4.0.3 as the north star. Three layers: Nervous System (OpenClaw), Brain (Claude Code), Soul (TELOS + memory). Don't reinvent — adapt.

### Agent-First Design
Every new capability starts as an agent spec, not code. Define the agent's purpose, inputs, outputs, and success criteria before writing a single line.

### Progressive Autonomy
Start with human-in-the-loop for every action. Earn trust by demonstrating reliability. Gradually expand autonomous authority as track record builds.

## Growth Strategy

### Build in Public (Eventually)
Document the journey. When PAI reaches 78/100, start sharing learnings. The content IS the marketing.

### Enterprise Bridge
Oracle Health engagement is the enterprise proving ground. What works there validates the platform for other enterprise customers.
