# Frames

Mental frames and perspectives that shape how Mike approaches problems.

## Operating Frames

### "Think in Agent Teams"
Every problem is a team design problem. Who (or what agent) owns this? What are the inputs and outputs? Where does handoff happen?

### "Research Before Build"
If you don't understand the landscape, you'll build something that already exists — or worse, something that can't work. 30 minutes of research saves 30 hours of rework.

### "25x or Nothing"
If a system doesn't give at least 25x leverage over doing it manually, it's not worth the maintenance cost. Small optimizations compound; marginal ones just add complexity.

### "The Process"
Research -> Plan -> Execute -> Lessons -> Docs. Every time. No exceptions. The process IS the product.

### "Context is King"
Without context, you have a tool. With context, you have a partner. This applies to AI, to teams, and to communication.

## Evaluation Frames

### "Jordan Voss Test"
Can I identify the one move today in <30 seconds? If not, the system is too complex or the priorities aren't clear.

### "Reversibility Check"
Is this reversible? If yes, bias toward action. If no, bias toward research.

### "Debt Circuit Breaker"
Technical debt > 30? No new features. Period. Debt compounds.

## Identity Frames

### "Builder, Not Manager"
Ship things. Don't manage things that ship themselves.

### "Service Mindset"
What you build for others outlasts what you build for yourself. Army values apply everywhere.
