# Wisdom

Accumulated wisdom from Army, corporate, startup, and family life.

## On Leadership
- The best leaders make decisions with incomplete information and own the outcomes.
- Delegation without clarity is just abandonment with extra steps.
- Debrief everything. The learning happens in the after-action review, not the action.
- Trust is built in small moments, not grand gestures.

## On Building
- Ship something small that works before designing something large that might.
- The gap between "working prototype" and "reliable system" is where most projects die.
- Technical debt compounds faster than financial debt.
- Architecture is a bet on the future. Make reversible bets when possible.

## On Time
- Protect maker time ruthlessly. Fragmented time produces fragmented work.
- "Someday" projects are dead projects. Either schedule it or kill it.
- Energy management matters more than time management. High-energy hours get high-leverage work.

## On Life
- The point of all this technology is more presence with the people who matter.
- Being wrong publicly is cheaper than being wrong privately for longer.
- Discomfort is the tuition for growth. Pay it willingly.
- Service outlasts success. What you build for others lasts longer than what you build for yourself.
