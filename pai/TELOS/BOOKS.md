# Books

Books that shaped Mike's thinking. Organized by domain.

## Leadership & Military
- *Extreme Ownership* — Jocko Willink & Leif Babin (own everything, make no excuses)
- *Team of Teams* — General Stanley McChrystal (shared consciousness, decentralized execution)
- *Turn the Ship Around!* — L. David Marquet (leader-leader, not leader-follower)
- *Good to Great* — Jim Collins (disciplined people, disciplined thought, disciplined action)

## Strategy & Business
- *The Lean Startup* — Eric Ries (build-measure-learn, validated learning)
- *Zero to One* — Peter Thiel (secrets, monopoly, definite optimism)
- *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* — Ben Horowitz (no recipe for hard decisions)
- *Playing to Win* — A.G. Lafley & Roger Martin (where to play, how to win)

## Technology & AI
- *The Pragmatic Programmer* — Hunt & Thomas (tracer bullets, DRY, orthogonality)
- *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* — Martin Kleppmann (systems thinking for data)
- *Life 3.0* — Max Tegmark (AI futures and what matters)

## Thinking & Decision Making
- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — Daniel Kahneman (System 1 vs System 2)
- *The Art of War* — Sun Tzu (strategy is older than technology)
- *Antifragile* — Nassim Taleb (gain from disorder, optionality)
