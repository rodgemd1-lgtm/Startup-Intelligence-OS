# Growth & Scaling — PLG, Viral Loops & Network Effects

> Growth frameworks, scaling strategies, and expansion playbooks from $1M → $10M ARR.

---

## 1. Growth Framework: 0 → $10M ARR

### The 4 Stages

| Stage | Revenue | Focus | Key Metric |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Explore** | $0-$100K | Find one channel that works | Weekly growth rate |
| **Validate** | $100K-$1M | Make it repeatable | CAC payback, retention |
| **Scale** | $1M-$5M | Pour fuel on what works | MoM growth, NRR |
| **Expand** | $5M-$10M | Add channels, segments, products | Rule of 40, NRR |

### Growth Levers at Each Stage
```
$0-$100K:  Product quality + founder hustle + community
$100K-$1M: Content/SEO + PLG + outbound
$1M-$5M:  Paid acquisition + partnerships + sales team
$5M-$10M: Brand + international + enterprise + platform
```

## 2. Product-Led Growth (PLG)

### PLG Metrics Stack
| Metric | Target | How to Improve |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Signup → Activated** | 40-60% | Reduce friction, improve onboarding |
| **Activated → Paid** | 5-15% | Demonstrate value, smart paywall |
| **Free → Team** | 10-20% of paid | Collaboration features, sharing |
| **Monthly → Annual** | 30-50% | Discount + commitment features |
| **Net Revenue Retention** | 110-140% | Usage-based expansion, upsell |

### PLG Growth Loops
1. **Viral loop**: User invites teammates → teammates invite others
2. **Content loop**: Users create content → content attracts new users
3. **Data network effect**: More users → better AI → more users
4. **Integration loop**: Integrate with tools → appear in marketplaces
5. **UGC loop**: Templates, workflows shared → discovery → signups

### PLG Case Studies (2026)
| Company | Key PLG Mechanic | Result |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Lovable** | Free tier → viral sharing | 8M users, $200M ARR |
| **Cursor** | Free trial → habit formation | $100M+ ARR |
| **Bolt.new** | Free usage → upgrade limits | $40M ARR in 5 months |
| **Figma** | Multiplayer → team adoption | $1B+ revenue, IPO |
| **Canva** | Free → share → invite | 260M MAU, $3.5B ARR |

## 3. Viral Mechanics

### Viral Coefficient (K-factor)
```
K = (invitations per user) × (conversion rate of invitations)
```
- K > 1 = exponential viral growth
- K > 0.5 = good supplementary growth
- K < 0.3 = not a viral product

### Viral Loop Types
| Type | Mechanism | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Inherent** | Product requires sharing | Slack, Figma |
| **Artificial** | Incentivized sharing | Dropbox referrals |
| **Content** | Created content is discoverable | Notion templates |
| **Social proof** | "Made with X" badge | Lovable, Webflow |
| **Network** | More users = more value | Marketplace, social |

## 4. Scaling Channels

### Channel Evaluation Framework
| Channel | CAC | Scalability | Speed | Control |
|---------|-----|-------------|-------|---------|
| **Content/SEO** | Low | High | Slow | High |
| **PLG/Viral** | Very low | Very high | Medium | Medium |
| **Paid search** | Medium | Medium | Fast | High |
| **Paid social** | Medium | Medium | Fast | High |
| **Outbound sales** | High | Medium | Medium | High |
| **Partnerships** | Low | Medium | Slow | Low |
| **Marketplace** | Low | High | Medium | Low |
| **Community** | Very low | Medium | Slow | Medium |
| **PR/Media** | Low | Low | Fast | Low |
| **Events** | High | Low | Fast | Medium |

### The "Triple Double" Framework ($1M-$10M)
1. **Double down** on your #1 channel (50% of spend)
2. **Develop** your #2 channel (30% of spend)
3. **Discover** new channels through experiments (20% of spend)

## 5. Network Effects

### Types of Network Effects
| Type | Description | Strength |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Direct** | More users → more valuable for each user | Strongest |
| **Cross-side** | More of group A → more valuable for group B | Strong |
| **Data** | More usage → better product (AI/ML) | Strong |
| **Platform** | More integrations → more valuable ecosystem | Medium |
| **Social** | Identity/reputation tied to platform | Medium |

### Building Data Network Effects for AI
1. Every user interaction improves the model
2. Aggregated data creates insights no individual has
3. Personalization improves with more data per user
4. Benchmark data attracts new users
5. Content/templates created by users attract new users

## 6. International Expansion

### When to Go International
- Strong product-market fit in home market
- $2M+ ARR with solid retention
- Product is language/culture-agnostic (or can be adapted)
- Demand signals from international users

### Expansion Checklist
- [ ] Localization (UI, docs, support)
- [ ] Local payment methods (Stripe supports 135+ currencies)
- [ ] GDPR compliance (EU)
- [ ] Local entity if needed (tax implications)
- [ ] Time zone-appropriate support
- [ ] Local marketing and content
